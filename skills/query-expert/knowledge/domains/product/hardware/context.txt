Device Types and Identifiers:
- reader_type: Hardware device types (X2, T2, T3, R12, S1, S3, etc.)
- reader_type_key: Specific hardware model versions (e.g., X2A, X2B, T2A) If the exact version is not specified, X2 defaults to X2A and T2 defaults to T2A. 
- Reader_type_family: Higher level grouping of related reader types (e.g. S1, S3, R12 S1 Bundle all grouped as Stand) 
- fatp_serial: Unique serial number for hardware devices (also referred to as: serial number, FATP, or unique identifier for a hardware/reader type). 
- unit_token: Unique identifier for seller's (or business’) physical location 
- merchant_token: Unique identifier for the merchant/seller
- WOM: Week of Manufacture (derived from first 3 characters of FATP serial)
- Pentagon_merchant_token: alternative identifier of merchant used in Shop Tables 

Device Details: 
- vertical or app_name: Point of Sale (POS) product used for the transaction or app app_version: point of sale (POS) version 
- os_name: Name of the operating system (os vs android) 
- os_version: Full version number of the operating system 
- firmware version: the version of the firmware 

Device Categories:
- Squid devices: X2, T2, and T3
- Stand devices: S1, S3
- Reader devices: R4, R12

Data Update Frequency:
- ES2 tables are typically refreshed once per day
- All HDM tables are refreshed once per day 

Device Purchase and Distribution
- Shop purchases can be tracked through app_hardware.hdm.fact_shop_line_item
- Third-party retail purchases (Amazon, Best Buy) aren't directly tracked. A device is sent to the retailer and sits on the shelf. We do not know about this device until the seller turns it on / takes a payment (If it is still on the retailed shelf or seller never turned it on, we do not know about this device) 
- Can identify device usage through ToF/BoF in product_events

Important Metrics:
- Payment methods: dips, swipes, taps (counts and GPV)
- Performance metrics: connectivity, transactions, errors, crashes 
- Firmware metrics: adoption rates, update success/failure
- Warranty and returns tracking
- Transaction success rates and error tracking

Performance Metrics - Additional Detail
- Payment success metrics include:
  - Approval rates (USB, BLE, dip)
  - eMoney success rates
  - Quick chip speeds
- Crash data includes:
  - BLE crashes
  - Telemetry coredumps
  - SPE crashes (in and outside payment flows)
- Connectivity metrics track:
  - Connects/disconnects
  - Plugs/unplugs
  - Unresponsive errors

Two ways to measure active devices:
- Payment-based: Device took a payment
- Telemetry-based: Device sent heartbeat signals
