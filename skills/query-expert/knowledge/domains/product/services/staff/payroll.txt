1. BUSINESS OVERVIEW
================================================================================

WHAT IS SQUARE PAYROLL?
Square Payroll is a full-service payroll SaaS product that serves both W2 
employees ("full service") and contractors (1099). The business grows slowly 
but retains very well, with strong seasonal patterns driven by "Switching 
Season" in January when employers switch payroll providers.

KEY BUSINESS METRICS:
- ARPU: Average Revenue Per Unit (per employer)
- EE/ER: Average employees per employer (drives ARPU)
- Billing Structure: $35/month base fee + $6 per distinct employee paid 
  monthly (billed in arrears)
- Primary Focus: Most metrics are indexed on "full service" (W2) employers 
  rather than contractor-only employers

REVENUE & BILLING:
- Billing in Arrears: Payroll is billed after service delivery, which causes 
  it to appear differently in revenue tables compared to other Square products 
  that bill upfront
- Revenue Recognition: Due to arrears billing, revenue timing may not align 
  with other product lines
- Mailing Fees: $3/employee for W2/1099 forms starting Jan 2024 creates 
  revenue spikes in January

BUSINESS CATEGORIZATION:
- "audience" = audience_predicted field
- "business category" or "sub category" = audience_predicted / 
  sub_audience_predicted
- "F&B" = "food_and_beverage" audience
- "SMB+" = all GPV segments SMB and higher (excludes Micro and Inactive)

DATA SOURCES FOR BUSINESS CONTEXT:
- Audiences: app_bi.hexagon.vdim_merchant
- Revenue Segments: app_bi.hexagon.vdim_merchant_gpv_segment

2. KEY DATA SOURCES & HERO TABLES
================================================================================

DATA STORAGE LOCATIONS:
Payroll data in Snowflake is primarily stored in two locations:
- payroll.raw_oltp schema: Contains raw data views that mirror the production 
  database
- app_payroll.app_payroll schema: Contains analytics tables produced by ETL 
  processes

While both can be valuable, operations work typically requires the raw data 
from payroll.raw_oltp.

HERO ANALYTICS TABLES:
These tables represent the most commonly used and reliable data sources for 
payroll analysis. Start with these tables when beginning analysis.

1. APP_PAYROLL.APP_PAYROLL.PAYROLL_STUB_SUMMARY
   - Primary table for payroll payment analysis
   - Pre-filtered to COMPLETED runs with non-deleted stubs
   - Contains detailed pay statement information
   - 89 active users, VERIFIED status
   - Updated by: DGOLE

2. APP_PAYROLL.APP_PAYROLL.PAYROLL_EMPLOYER_LIFETIME_SUMMARY
   - Comprehensive employer metrics throughout Payroll journey
   - Primary source for funnel analysis
   - 114 active users, VERIFIED status
   - Updated by: DGOLE

3. APP_PAYROLL.APP_PAYROLL.PAYROLL_EMPLOYER_MONTHLY_SUMMARY_SUB2
   - Monthly snapshots of employer metrics
   - Subscription status, billing details, feature usage
   - 55 active users, VERIFIED status
   - Updated by: PWEIR

4. APP_PA.APP_SQS.EMPLOYEES
   - Employee data across Square Staff and Payroll
   - 64 active users, VERIFIED status
   - Updated by: PAZAR

RAW PRODUCTION TABLES:
- PAYROLL.RAW_OLTP.EMPLOYERS (employer records)
- PAYROLL.RAW_OLTP.EMPLOYEE_FACETS (one row per employee)
- PAYROLL.RAW_OLTP.STUBS (individual pay statements)
- PAYROLL.RAW_OLTP.RUNS (payroll run metadata)
- PAYROLL.RAW_OLTP.TASKS (setup and operational tasks)
- PAYROLL.RAW_OLTP.REPORTS (reporting data)
- PAYROLL.RAW_OLTP.EARNINGS (hours, rates, tips, commissions)

UNDERSTANDING THE RAW DATA MODEL:
The raw payroll data in Snowflake is a daily replication of production data 
with a one-to-one mapping:
- Each Ruby-on-Rails class becomes a table/view
- Fields within each class become columns
- Each row represents a specific object instance

This direct mapping makes it easier to connect Snowflake queries with the 
actual product behavior.

3. PRODUCT FEATURES & TERMINOLOGY
================================================================================

SETUP PROCESS:

Phase 1: Onboarding (Linear Flow)
Low-friction data collection:
1. Business name
2. Business address
3. Phone number
4. Approximate number of employees

Phase 2: Setup (Any Order)
Required tasks before first payroll run:
- Enter federal tax information (EIN)
- Enter state tax information*
- Setup authorization/identity verification
- Add team member for payroll
- Link bank account
- Select payment cadence and initial pay run date*
*Not required for contractor-only employers

PAY SPEED OPTIONS:

Employer-Facing Pay Speeds (Run Level):
- "Instant Payment" / "1DP" / "SQUARE_BALANCE_FUNDED": Money moves in 1 day. 
  Must be funded from Square Balance / Square Checking
- "Two-Day-Pay" / "2DP": Money moves in 2 days. Employer must pass risk checks
- "Four-Day-Pay" / "4DP": Money moves in 4 days. Fallback option for risky 
  employers. More than half of employers now use faster pay speeds (2DP or 1DP)

Employee-Facing Pay Speeds (Stub/Employee Level):
- "Instant Deposit" / "ID": Employees receive paychecks sooner than standard 
  timing
  * Fee Structure: 1.75% fee, or free if paid to Cash App
  * Timing Benefit: 2 days sooner for 4DP employers, 12-24 hours sooner for 
    2DP employers
- "On Demand Pay" / "ODP": Employees access partial paycheck before pay period 
  ends
  * Status: Deprecated in late 2024 (will appear in historical data)

4. GROWTH & FUNNEL METRICS
================================================================================

FUNNEL & KEY MILESTONES:

Top of Funnel:
1. Started Onboarding (SOB): Completed first page of payroll intake questions
2. Completed Onboarding: Finished linear intake questions flow (misleading 
   name - setup continues)
3. Free Trial Start (FT): Main "top of funnel" metric indicating product 
   discovery and intent

Bottom of Funnel:
- Sent First Run (SFR): Ultimate conversion event when employer sends their 
  first payroll run
- Gross Adds: Full service (W2) SFRs specifically

CONVERSION FLOW BY EMPLOYER TYPE:

Contractor-Only Employers:
SOB → Completed Onboarding → Released into product for setup → SFR

Full Service Employers (W2):
SOB → Completed Onboarding → Free Trial Start → Setup → SFR (Gross Add)

SEASONALITY & TIMING:

Switching Season:
- Primary Period: January
- Driver: Easier to switch payroll providers at calendar year break
- Impact: Significant influx of new employers in January

Conversion Timing:
- Most recent SFRs may still be "curing" (completing setup process)
- Use appropriate lookback periods for conversion rate analysis

ANALYSIS BEST PRACTICES:

Standard Analysis Windows:
- Setup Completion Rates: 7-day conversion window starting from either 
  "Free Trial Start" or "Started Onboarding"
- Sent First Run (SFR): 30-day conversion window
  * Starting point: Usually FT start
  * Rationale: Normal for employers to take time setting up payroll, but SFR 
    is the "sticky" action that indicates true product adoption
  * Business conviction: SFR is the right metric for counting conversions and 
    new users

Denominators for Conversion Analysis:
- Primary: Free Trial Start (for setup step completion rates)
- Alternative: Started Onboarding (for broader funnel analysis)

Comparison Methodology:
- Primary: Year-over-Year (YoY) comparisons due to strong seasonality
- Free Trial Metrics: YoY valid only from May 2025 forward

Key Conversion Rates to Monitor:
- SOB → Completed Onboarding (7-day window)
- Completed Onboarding → Free Trial Start (7-day window)
- Free Trial Start → Setup Step Completion (7-day window)
- Free Trial Start → SFR/Gross Add (30-day window)

PAYROLL.RAW_OLTP.RUNS - Payroll Run Metadata
============================================
Primary Key: TOKEN, ID
Verification: VERIFIED
Updated by: DGOLE
Total Users: 112

Description: Contains comprehensive payroll run metadata including period dates,
payment schedules, and processing statuses.

KEY COLUMNS:
- TOKEN (text): Unique identifier for the payroll run
- ID (number): Numeric identifier for the payroll run
- EMPLOYER_ID (number): Foreign key to PAYROLL.RAW_OLTP.EMPLOYERS
- PERIOD_START (date): Start date of the payroll period
- PERIOD_END (date): End date of the payroll period
- PAY_DATE (date): Date when employees are paid
- PAYOUT_DATE (date): Date when the payout is scheduled to occur
- SENT_TIME (timestamp_ntz): Timestamp when the payroll run was sent
- QUEUED_TIME (timestamp_ntz): Timestamp when payroll run was queued
- STATUS (text): Current status (COMPLETED, EDITING, PENDING)
- RUN_CADENCE (text): Frequency (WEEKLY, BIWEEKLY, SEMIMONTHLY, MONTHLY)
- PAYOUT_METHOD (text): Method used (DIRECT_DEPOSIT, MANUAL_CHECK)
- IS_OFF_CYCLE (boolean): Indicates if outside regular payroll schedule
- IS_CONTRACTOR (boolean): Indicates if this is a contractor payroll run
- PAYMENT_TYPE (text): Type (REGULAR, SUPPLEMENTAL_BONUS, SUPPLEMENTAL_OTHER)
- REASON_FOR_EARLY_PAYOUT (text): Indicates PAY SPEED of the run
  * NULL = 4DP standard speed
  * TWO_DAY_DIRECT_DEPOSIT = 2DP speed
  * SQUARE_BALANCE_FUNDED = 1DP speed
  * HOLIDAY_PAY = special holiday processing
  * PAYEMPR ticket numbers = manual interventions
- SEND_SCHEDULE (text): Schedule for sending the payroll
  * REGULAR = fastest available not used
  * IMMEDIATE_TWO_DAY_DIRECT_DEPOSIT = 2DP fastest available
  * INSTANT = 1DP fastest available

IMPORTANT: For most payroll analysis, use APP_PAYROLL.APP_PAYROLL.PAYROLL_STUB_SUMMARY 
instead - it's a pre-joined, filtered analytics table that combines runs and 
stubs data with additional calculated fields and pre-filters to COMPLETED runs 
with non-deleted records.

- INSTANT_DEPOSIT_SUCCESS (boolean): Indicates if instant deposit was successful
- INSTANT_DEPOSIT_TYPE (text): Type of instant deposit used (CASH, DEBIT_CARD)
- PTO_AVAILABLE_HOURS (number): Number of paid time off hours available
- PTO_POLICY_NAME (text): Name of the paid time off policy applicable
- EMPLOYEE_ADDRESS_ID (number): Foreign key to PAYROLL.RAW_OLTP.ADDRESSES
- EMPLOYER_ADDRESS_ID (number): Foreign key to PAYROLL.RAW_OLTP.ADDRESSES

IMPORTANT: For most payroll analysis, use APP_PAYROLL.APP_PAYROLL.PAYROLL_STUB_SUMMARY

APP_PAYROLL.APP_PAYROLL.PAYROLL_STUB_SUMMARY - Analytics Table
===============================================================
Primary Key: STUB_ID
Verification: VERIFIED
Updated by: DGOLE
Total Users: 89

Description: The core analytics table for payroll analysis - pre-filtered to 
only include COMPLETED runs with non-deleted stubs and runs. This is the 
preferred table for most payroll analysis.

KEY COLUMNS:
- STUB_ID (text): Primary identifier for the pay stub
- EMPLOYEE_FACET_ID (text): Links to employee facet records
- EMPLOYEE_TOKEN (text): Unique identifier for the employee
- MERCHANT_TOKEN (text): Unique identifier for the merchant/business
- EMPLOYER_ID (text): Identifier for the employer
- RUN_ID (text): Identifier for the payroll run
- RUN_SENT_AT (timestamp_ntz): Timestamp when the payroll run was sent
- RUN_SENT_AT_PT (timestamp_ntz): Timestamp in Pacific Time
- PAY_DATE (date): Date when the employee is paid
- PAYOUT_DATE (date): Date when the payout is processed
- PAY_PERIOD_START (date): Start date of the pay period
- PAY_PERIOD_END (date): End date of the pay period
- WEEKS_IN_PAY_PERIOD (number): Number of weeks in the pay period
- RUN_CADENCE (text): Frequency (WEEKLY, BIWEEKLY, SEMIMONTHLY, MONTHLY)
- RUN_STATUS (text): Current status (pre-filtered to COMPLETED only)
- IS_CONTRACTOR (boolean): Indicates if the payee is a contractor
  * Use to distinguish between employee (W2) and contractor payment data
- IS_OFF_CYCLE (boolean): Indicates if the payroll run is off-cycle
- PAYOUT_METHOD (text): Method used (DIRECT_DEPOSIT, MANUAL_CHECK, NO_PAYOUT)
  * Cash App deposits count as direct deposits here
- PAYOUT_CATEGORIZATION (text): Categorization of the payout
  * dd_to_cash = direct deposit to Cash App
  * dd_to_noncash = direct deposit to traditional bank
  * id_to_cash = instant deposit to Cash App
  * id_to_noncash = instant deposit to traditional bank
  * manual_check = manual check
  * no_payout = no payout
- CASHAPP_PAYOUT_FLAG (number): Flag indicating if Cash App was used for payout
- GROSS_PAY_CENTS (number): Gross pay amount in cents
  * Always populated for both employees and contractors
  * For contractors, this equals contractor_pay_value_cents
- NET_PAY_VALUE_CENTS (number): Net pay amount after taxes and deductions
  * Only populated for employees (W2), always NULL for contractors
- CONTRACTOR_PAY_VALUE_CENTS (number): Contractor pay amount in cents
  * Only populated for contractors (1099), always NULL for employees
  * No tax withholdings applied
- PAYOUT_VALUE_CENTS (number): Total payout value in cents
  * The actual amount paid out to the payee
  * Equals net_pay_value_cents for employees
  * Equals contractor_pay_value_cents for contractors
- REGULAR_HOURS (number): Number of regular hours worked
- OVERTIME_HOURS (number): Number of overtime hours worked
- DOUBLE_TIME_HOURS (number): Number of double time hours worked
- SICK_LEAVE_HOURS (number): Number of sick leave hours used
- PTO_HOURS (number): Number of paid time off hours used
- CONTRACTOR_HOURS (number): Number of hours worked by contractor
- RATE_CENTS (number): Regular hourly rate in cents
- OVERTIME_RATE_CENTS (number): Overtime hourly rate in cents
- DOUBLE_TIME_RATE_CENTS (number): Double time hourly rate in cents
- PTO_RATE_CENTS (number): Paid time off hourly rate in cents
- REGULAR_PAY_CENTS (number): Regular pay amount in cents
- OVERTIME_PAY_CENTS (number): Overtime pay amount in cents
- DOUBLE_TIME_PAY_CENTS (number): Double time pay amount in cents
- PTO_PAY_CENTS (number): Paid time off pay amount in cents
- ADDITIONAL_CENTS (number): Additional pay amount in cents
- TOTAL_TIP_CENTS (number): Total tips amount in cents
- REGULAR_HOURS_PER_WEEK (number): Average regular hours worked per week
- REGULAR_PAY_CENTS_PER_WEEK (number): Average regular pay in cents per week
- REASON_FOR_EARLY_PAYOUT (text): Indicates the PAY SPEED of the run
  * TWO_DAY_DIRECT_DEPOSIT = 2DP speed
  * SQUARE_BALANCE_FUNDED = 1DP speed
  * NULL = Standard 4DP speed
  * HOLIDAY_PAY = special holiday processing
  * PAYEMPR ticket numbers = manual interventions
- SEND_SCHEDULE (text): Schedule for sending payroll
  * REGULAR = fastest available was NOT used
  * INSTANT = fastest available with 1DP speed
  * IMMEDIATE_TWO_DAY_DIRECT_DEPOSIT = fastest available with 2DP speed
- USED_FASTEST_AVAILABLE_PAY_SPEED (boolean): Indicates if fastest available 
  pay speed was used
  * When enabled, employees get paid earlier than scheduled based on when run 
    was queued
- INSTANT_DEPOSIT_ELIGIBLE (boolean): Indicates if eligible for instant deposit
- INSTANT_DEPOSIT_SUCCESS (boolean): Indicates if instant deposit was successful
- INSTANT_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT_CENTS (number): Amount of instant deposit in cents
  * Instant Deposit allows employees to receive paychecks sooner 
    (1.75% fee, free to Cash App)
- INSTANT_DEPOSIT_FEE_CENTS (number): Fee charged for instant deposit in cents
- ID_TO_CASH_ELIGIBLE (boolean): Indicates if eligible for ID to cash service
- ID_TO_CASH_SUCCESS (boolean): Indicates if ID to cash transaction was successful
- EMPLOYEE_RUN_ORDER (number): Chronological sequence number of payroll runs 
  for each employee
  * Filter to = 1 for employee's first run, = 2 for second run, etc.
  * ROW_NUMBER() ORDER BY run_sent_at ASC PARTITION BY employee_token
- EMPLOYEE_RUN_ORDER_DD_ONLY (number): Chronological sequence number of direct 
  deposit runs for each employee
  * Same logic as employee_run_order but only counting direct deposit payouts
- EMPLOYER_RUN_ORDER (number): Chronological sequence number of payroll runs 
  for each employer
  * Filter to = 1 for employer's first run, = 2 for second run, etc.
  * ROW_NUMBER() ORDER BY run_sent_at ASC PARTITION BY employer_id
- HAS_COMMUTER_BENEFIT (boolean): Indicates if employee has commuter benefits
- HAS_HEALTH_BENEFIT (boolean): Indicates if employee has health benefits
- HAS_RETIREMENT_BENEFIT (boolean): Indicates if employee has retirement benefits
- IS_FPD_ENABLED_RUN_SCHEDULE (boolean): Indicates if Flexible Pay Day (FPD) 
  is enabled
  * FPD feature launched in 2024 allowing employers to choose any weekday as 
    payday for weekly/biweekly cadence
  * Critical for merchant switching scenarios to maintain existing pay schedules
- RUN_PLATFORM_ESTIMATED (text): Estimated platform used (desktop, mobile)
- RUN_QUEUED_OS_NAME_ESTIMATED (text): Estimated OS where run was queued
  * Windows, Mac OS, iOS, Android, Chromium OS, Linux, Ubuntu, FreeBSD
- REVERT_TO_MC_CATEGRORIZATION (text): Categorization for revert to manual check
  * Tracks ACH failures and manual check reversions
  * employer_reverted_to_mc
  * R03 - No Account/Unable to Locate Account
  * R02 - Account Closed
  * R04 - Invalid Account Number Structure
  * failed_stub_deposit_to_cash
  * Other ACH Error Code
  * Plus INFERRED versions

PAYROLL FUNNEL ANALYSIS (7-DAY SETUP COMPLETION):
```sql
WITH funnel AS (
    SELECT 
        DATE_TRUNC('month', started_free_trial_at) AS cohort_month,
        COUNT(DISTINCT merchant_token) AS started_ft,
        COUNT(DISTINCT CASE 
            WHEN setup_ein <= DATEADD('day', 7, started_free_trial_at)
            THEN merchant_token END) AS completed_ein_7d,
        COUNT(DISTINCT CASE 
            WHEN setup_bank_account <= DATEADD('day', 7, started_free_trial_at)
            THEN merchant_token END) AS completed_bank_7d,
        COUNT(DISTINCT CASE 
            WHEN setup_w2_team_member <= DATEADD('day', 7, started_free_trial_at)
            THEN merchant_token END) AS completed_employee_7d
    FROM app_payroll.app_payroll.payroll_employer_lifetime_summary
    WHERE started_free_trial_at >= '2024-05-01'
    GROUP BY 1
)
SELECT 
    cohort_month,
    started_ft,
    completed_ein_7d,
    ROUND(100.0 * completed_ein_7d / started_ft, 2) AS ein_completion_rate_7d,
    completed_bank_7d,
    ROUND(100.0 * completed_bank_7d / started_ft, 2) AS bank_completion_rate_7d,
    completed_employee_7d,
    ROUND(100.0 * completed_employee_7d / started_ft, 2) AS employee_completion_rate_7d
FROM funnel
ORDER BY cohort_month;
```

SENT FIRST RUN CONVERSION (30-DAY WINDOW):
```sql
WITH sfr_conversion AS (
    SELECT 
        DATE_TRUNC('month', started_free_trial_at) AS cohort_month,
        COUNT(DISTINCT merchant_token) AS started_ft,
        COUNT(DISTINCT CASE 
            WHEN first_payrun_sent_at <= DATEADD('day', 30, started_free_trial_at)
            THEN merchant_token END) AS sfr_30d
    FROM app_payroll.app_payroll.payroll_employer_lifetime_summary
    WHERE started_free_trial_at >= '2024-05-01'
      AND started_free_trial_at <= CURRENT_DATE - 30  -- Allow 30 days to cure
    GROUP BY 1
)
SELECT 
    cohort_month,
    started_ft,
    sfr_30d,
    ROUND(100.0 * sfr_30d / started_ft, 2) AS sfr_conversion_rate_30d
FROM sfr_conversion
ORDER BY cohort_month;
```

GROSS PAY ANALYSIS BY PAY SPEED:
```sql
SELECT 
    DATE_TRUNC('month', run_sent_at) AS month,
    CASE 
        WHEN reason_for_early_payout = 'SQUARE_BALANCE_FUNDED' THEN '1DP'
        WHEN reason_for_early_payout = 'TWO_DAY_DIRECT_DEPOSIT' THEN '2DP'
        WHEN reason_for_early_payout IS NULL THEN '4DP'
        ELSE 'Other'

    COUNT(DISTINCT merchant_token) AS employers,
    COUNT(DISTINCT employee_token) AS employees,

```

8. CASH APP INTEGRATION
================================================================================

CASH APP LINKING METHODS:

Proper Linking (P2P):
- Linking Experience: Employee follows happy path and uses "magic" linking to 
  connect Cash App account
- Payout Process: P2P Transactions send money from Square master Cash App 
  account to employee's Cash App account
- Money Movement Data: Easy to see in payroll stubs and money movement data
- Linking Data: Complete and rigorous mapping of payout money movement data → 
  Square Payroll employee ID

Manual Linking (ACH):
- Linking Experience: Employee (or employer) adds Cash App account as direct 
  deposit using routing number and account number (just like any other bank)
- Payout Process: ACH transactions are used, just like direct deposit to 
  traditional bank
- Money Movement Data: Looks just like any other direct deposit payout in 
  payroll data; requires Cash App side data to identify
- Linking Data: Incomplete (but pretty good) mapping using SQUASH mapping 
  dataset based on phone number and email

CASH APP METRICS:

PDA (Payroll Direct Active):
- Definition: Cash App user receiving $300+ monthly from Square Payroll
- P2P PDAs: ~75% of total PDAs
- ACH PDAs: ~25% of total PDAs
- Monthly Active Count (July 2025): ~24k total PDAs
- Growth: P2P has grown from ~15k to ~23k (Jan 2024 to July 2025)

Monetization:
- Square Payroll PDAs generate ~$550k gross profit per month for Cash App
- ~$23 per PDA per month
- P2P Monetization Rate: 1.17% (estimated)
- ACH Monetization Rate: 1.30%
- General PDA Monetization Rate: 1.26%

Monetization Breakdown:
- P2P PDAs: 39% from Instant Deposit, 36% from Cash Card, 22% from Borrow
- ACH PDAs: 16% from Instant Deposit, 53% from Cash Card, 32% from Borrow
- General PDAs: 11% from Instant Deposit, 51% from Cash Card, 32% from Borrow

STRATEGIC IMPORTANCE:
- $8B+ addressable annual inflows from Square to Cash App
- Daily Tip Payouts integration (launched 2025)
- Employee Network strategy: Move employee experience into Cash App
- Team Member Accounts becoming Cash App accounts
- Team App experience as Cash App applet

EMPLOYER-EMPLOYEE ONBOARDING:
- Employers can invite employees to self-onboard OR enter direct deposit info 
  on behalf of employee
- Proper Cash App linking only possible through self-onboarding
- Baseline self-onboarding rate: ~62% (38% employer-onboarded)
- For ACH users with $300+: 53% had first payout method added by employer 
  (higher than baseline)
- ~15% of ACH-actives driven by employers' inability to properly link Cash App
- ~14% of ACH PDAs driven by employees used to linking Cash App manually from 
  previous jobs
