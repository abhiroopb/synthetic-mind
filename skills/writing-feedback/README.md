# Writing feedback

> Write and format performance feedback using the IBB (Impact, Behaviors, Betterment) model for self-reflections, peer reviews, and manager evaluations.

## What it does

Helps employees write effective performance feedback structured into three sections: Impact (measurable outcomes and results), Behaviors (how the person showed up — collaboration, communication, decision-making), and Betterment (growth areas and actionable development suggestions). It anchors feedback to career ladder expectations for the person's discipline and level, checks for bias, and ensures coverage of the full review window.

## Usage

Tell the skill who the feedback is for, their role and level, and provide specific examples and accomplishments to include.

- "Write feedback for [person]"
- "Help me draft my self-reflection"
- "Write a peer review for [person]"

## Examples

- `"Write a peer review for a L5 engineer — they led the offline payments project and mentored two junior engineers"`
- `"Help me draft my self-reflection — I shipped cash rounding, ran 3 experiments, and presented at the all-hands"`
- `"Write a manager evaluation for a L4 designer who consistently exceeds expectations"`

## Why it was created

Performance feedback is high-stakes writing that people struggle with — it's hard to be specific, structured, and fair under time pressure. This skill enforces the IBB model, eliminates bias-prone language, and grounds everything in concrete examples rather than vague generalizations.
