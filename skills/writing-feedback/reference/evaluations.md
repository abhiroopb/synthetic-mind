# Performance Evaluations

## IBB Model

Performance is evaluated through three lenses:

- **Impact (What):** Measures effectiveness in achieving expected outcomes relative to the role. Includes contributions toward team goals, customer needs, and strategic priorities.
- **Behaviors (How):** Evaluates how work is done. Focuses on observable actions, attitudes, and approaches demonstrated daily, assessed against company-wide and role-specific expectations.
- **Betterment (Improvement):** Focuses on making oneself, the team, or the company better. Includes increasing efficiency, effectiveness, engagement, velocity, and creativity.

## Performance Rating Scale (Four-Point)

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Consistently Exceeds Expectations** | Consistently exceeded role expectations; maintained sustained excellence; demonstrated outsized competence/impact; served as a leader/mentor even without official reporting lines. |
| **Consistently Meets Expectations** | Consistently met and sometimes exceeded expectations; reliable, sustained performance; fulfilled core responsibilities and objectives; delivered solid quality work and met deadlines. |
| **Inconsistently Meets Expectations** | Frequently met but sometimes fell short of expectations; fluctuating performance; gaps in work quality, follow-through, or impact; requires additional development and support. |
| **Below Expectations** | Consistently fell short of role expectations; failed to demonstrate sustained progress; work quality and impact were below the expected level; ongoing performance gaps. |

**Guided distribution:** ~20% Exceeds, ~70% Meets, ~10% Inconsistently Meets/Below.

## Evaluation Process (Year-End)

- **Manager Submission:** Managers select a rating and write an open-text summary (no character limit).
- **Calibration:** Leadership reviews ratings to ensure consistency across teams.
- **Text Refinement:** After calibration, managers can refine the text summary but cannot change the finalized rating.
- **Visibility:** Evaluations become visible to employees in your performance review system.
- **Conversations:** Performance discussions take place between managers and employees.

## Manager Best Practices

- **Continuous Alignment:** Regularly discuss success through the IBB lens using career ladders, OKRs, and 1:1 notes.
- **Snapshot Perspective:** Ratings are snapshots in time, not permanent labels.
- **No Surprises:** Leverage continuous feedback loops so the formal evaluation is a summary of ongoing discussions.
- **Holistic Review:** Gather feedback from peers, previous managers, and cross-functional partners.
- **Anchor in Ladders:** Use career ladders to define the "how" (behaviors) and specific role goals to define the "what" (impact).

## Special Scenarios

### Recently Promoted Employees
- Promotions effective Jan 1: Typically "Exceeds" (reflects exceptional performance warranting promotion).
- Promotions effective Oct 1: Typically "Meets" (reflects ramping up in new role).

### Leave of Absence (LOA)
- Evaluate based only on time worked; LOA must not negatively impact the rating.
- Full year LOA: No evaluation required.

### New Hires
- Employees hired on or before October 1 are eligible for year-end evaluation.
- "Meets Expectations" is generally appropriate; "Exceeds" is unlikely given limited timeframe.

## Writing the Evaluation

The evaluation should:
- Be grounded in signals from peers, previous managers, and cross-functional partners.
- Reflect the full year of performance (H1 and H2), not just recent wins (recency bias).
- Use the IBB lens to structure the summary clearly.
- Be clear, specific, and free of vague or coded language.
