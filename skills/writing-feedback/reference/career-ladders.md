# Career Ladders Reference

## Engineering Career Ladder

| Level | Title | Primary Focus |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| L3 | Software Engineer I | Developing technical proficiency and contributing to team tasks with guidance. |
| L4 | Software Engineer II | Independent execution of features and small projects; increasing ownership. |
| L5 | Senior Software Engineer | Leading medium-to-large projects; technical design ownership and mentoring. |
| L6 | Staff Software Engineer | Strategic technical leadership across multiple teams or a large functional area. |
| L7 | Principal Software Engineer | Organization-wide impact; setting 1-3 year technical visions and roadmaps. |
| L8 | Distinguished Engineer | Industry-leading expertise; influencing company-wide strategy and culture. |

### Engineering IBB Examples by Level

**L3 (Software Engineer I)**
- Expectation: Consistently delivers assigned tasks on time; learns the codebase.
- Impact example: Delivered the UI components for the new onboarding flow ahead of schedule.
- Behavior example: Proactively asked clarifying questions when requirements were ambiguous.
- Betterment example: Focus on deep-diving into the backend services to understand the full data flow.

**L4 (Software Engineer II)**
- Expectation: Owns end-to-end features; provides quality code reviews.
- Impact example: Refactored the payment retry logic, resulting in a 2% increase in transaction success rates.
- Behavior example: Demonstrated high ownership by monitoring the rollout and responding to alerts immediately.
- Betterment example: Start leading technical design discussions for smaller sub-systems.

**L5 (Senior Software Engineer)**
- Expectation: Multi-month project leadership; mentors others; identifies technical debt.
- Impact example: Led the migration from the legacy monolith to a new microservice, reducing p99 latency by 150ms.
- Behavior example: Effectively managed stakeholder expectations across Product and Design during the migration.
- Betterment example: Look for opportunities to influence technical standards across the broader engineering organization.

**L6 (Staff Software Engineer)**
- Expectation: Solves complex, ambiguous problems; influences multiple teams; technical gatekeeper.
- Impact example: Designed the cross-service authentication framework used by the entire business unit.
- Behavior example: Mentored three Senior Engineers, two of whom were promoted this cycle.
- Betterment example: Focus on simplifying the long-term technical debt roadmap to improve developer velocity for all teams.

**L7 (Principal Software Engineer)**
- Expectation: Strategic influence; technical "North Star" for the organization.
- Impact example: Initiated and led the "Cloud Cost Optimization" project, saving the org $2M annually.
- Behavior example: Cultivated a culture of high-quality documentation and design docs across the org.
- Betterment example: Increase external technical branding (blog posts, conferences) to assist in high-level recruiting.

## Product Career Ladder

| Level | Title | Core Focus |
|-------|-------|------------|
| L2 | Associate Product Manager | Execution of well-defined tasks; high guidance. |
| L3 | Product Manager | Ownership of specific features/sub-products; moderate guidance. |
| L4 | Senior Product Manager | Independence; ownership of a product area or surface. |
| L5 | Staff Product Manager | Strategic direction for a pod/multiple teams; mentorship. |
| L6 | Senior Staff Product Manager | Strategic leadership for a large pillar or domain; business unit influence. |
| L7 | Principal Product Manager | High-level IC role influencing long-term business strategy. |
| L8 | Group Product Manager | Leadership of large organizations and multi-pillar strategy. |

### Product Core Competencies
- **Product Vision & Strategy:** Defining "the why" and aligning with the company's mission.
- **Product Execution:** Translating strategy into roadmaps and delivering quality features.
- **Customer Empathy:** Deep understanding of user pain points and needs.
- **Analytical Rigor:** Using data to drive decisions and measure success.
- **Influence & Leadership:** Cross-functional collaboration and team cultural contribution.

### Product Level Expectations

**L2 (Associate PM)**
- Execution: Delivers features on time with clear PRDs; identifies and flags blockers.
- Strategy: Understands the team roadmap; learns how to prioritize based on goals.
- Leadership: Participates in team rituals; communicates clearly with immediate peers.

**L3 (PM)**
- Execution: Operates independently on feature sets; manages full lifecycle from discovery to launch.
- Strategy: Contributes to quarterly planning; uses metrics to justify prioritization.
- Leadership: Builds strong relationships with engineering and design counterparts.

**L4 (Senior PM)**
- Execution: Handles complex, ambiguous projects with multiple stakeholders; high quality of craft.
- Strategy: Defines strategy for a product area; identifies new opportunities beyond current roadmap.
- Leadership: Mentors junior PMs; influences cross-functional partners without authority.

**L5 (Staff PM)**
- Execution: Optimized for team-wide velocity; creates scalable processes.
- Strategy: Sets 1-2 year visions; aligns pod goals with Business Unit objectives.
- Leadership: Recognized as a leader within the BU; drives cultural improvements.

### Product IBB Examples

**Constructive (L4 PM):**
- Intent: "Your intent was to provide a thorough update on the project status during the leadership review."
- Behavior: "However, you spent 15 minutes on technical implementation details instead of focusing on the launch risks."
- Impact: "The result was that the stakeholders didn't have enough time to provide feedback on the mitigation plan, delaying our decision by a week."

**Positive (L3 PM):**
- Intent: "Your intent was to improve team alignment during the sprint transition."
- Behavior: "I noticed you created a centralized dashboard that tracks cross-functional dependencies in real-time."
- Impact: "This reduced our weekly sync time by 20 minutes and prevented two potential shipping delays this month."

## Product Marketing Career Ladder

| Level | Title | Focus |
|-------|-------|-------|
| L3 | Associate PMM (APMM) | Execution and learning the PMM playbook. |
| L4 | PMM | Ownership of specific features/launches and cross-functional coordination. |
| L5 | Senior PMM | Leading complex programs and influencing product roadmaps. |
| L6 | PMM Lead | Strategic leadership for product pillars and team mentorship. |
| L7 | PMM Director | Organizational strategy, vision, and systemic problem-solving. |
| L8 | Senior PMM Director | Executive-level vision and long-term organizational impact. |

### PMM Core Competencies
1. **Product & Marketing Strategy:** From market research (L3) to long-term strategic vision (L6+).
2. **GTM Execution & Operations:** From tactical tasks (L3) to defining the operational playbook (L6+).
3. **Leadership & Influence:** From immediate team collaboration (L3) to shaping the PMM organization (L7+).
4. **Impact & Scale:** From defined KPIs (L3) to business-wide outcomes (L6+).

### PMM IBB Alignment
- For L3/L4: Focus feedback on quality of execution and coordination (Behaviors).
- For L5/L6: Focus feedback on strategic influence and program complexity (Impact).
- For L7+: Focus feedback on organizational leadership and long-term vision (Intent/Impact).

## Design Career Ladder

### Individual Contributor (IC) Path

| Level | Title |
|-------|-------|
| L3 | Designer |
| L4 | Designer |
| L5 | Senior Designer |
| L6 | Staff Designer |
| L7 | Principal Designer |
| L8 | Principal Designer |

### Design Competency Mapping
| Competency | Framework Equivalent |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| Craft Mastery | Craft (Impact) |
| Product Impact | Strategy & Velocity (Impact) |
| Collaboration | Partnership (Behaviors) |
| Leadership | Ownership (Behaviors) & People (Betterment) |
| Systems Thinking | Included within Craft (Impact) |
| Cross-functional Influence | Influence (Behaviors) |

### Design IC Level Expectations

**L3 (Designer)**
- Influence: Actively contributes in team settings; explains work well.
- Collaboration: Works with lead to set expectations; open to feedback.
- Ownership: Owns tasks with well-defined goals; seeks guidance on prioritization.
- Craft: Foundational AI fluency; follows best practices; developing taste.
- Product Impact: Learning how work drives metrics; delivers work on time.
- Systems Thinking: Learning to leverage existing systems and standards.

**L4 (Designer)**
- Influence: Shares work effectively; impacts customer experiences for small projects.
- Collaboration: Proactively works with manager to improve skills; suggests AI use.
- Ownership: Owns small projects with well-defined scope; works more independently.
- Craft: Maintains high standards; refines use of systems and emerging tech.
- Product Impact: Contributes strategic ideas; finds opportunities to "build first, then learn."
- Systems Thinking: Refines ability to leverage systems and standards.

**L5 (Senior Designer)**
- Influence: Articulates rationale in presentations; manages stakeholder expectations for large projects.
- Collaboration: Demonstrates transparency; promotes customer-centric solutions and automation.
- Ownership: Owns large projects or multiple small initiatives; defines scope and outcomes.
- Craft: Expertly leverages new tools and systems; builds cohesive end-to-end experiences.
- Product Impact: Solid understanding of AI/automation and customer insights to drive business strategy.
- Systems Thinking: Expertly leveraging systems, standards, and automation tools.

**L6 (Staff Designer)**
- Influence: Influences decisions and promotes innovation for a product area or larger projects.
- Collaboration: Fosters trust with cross-functional leaders; offers actionable feedback.
- Ownership: Takes ownership of complex initiatives with minimal guidance; knows when to escalate.
- Craft: Contributes meaningfully to systems and automation; maintains a high bar for self/others.
- Product Impact: Influences roadmaps and strategy; pushes partners to "build first" to solve problems.
- Systems Thinking: Contributing to systems and defining standards.

**L7 (Principal Designer)**
- Influence: Key leader for critical initiatives; influences outcomes for a brand or large domain.
- Collaboration: Cultivates mutually respectful relationships with leadership peers; resolves conflicts.
- Ownership: Takes full accountability for multiple complex initiatives; uncovers new business opportunities.
- Craft: Recognized expert; sought after for innovative approaches and meticulous detail.
- Product Impact: Proven track record of developing successful long-term, inspiring visions and strategies.
- Systems Thinking: Key contributor in defining and upholding systems and standards across a brand.

**L8 (Principal Designer)**
- Influence: Trusted senior leader consulted on top company priorities; impacts large-scale organizations.
- Collaboration: Senior leadership partner; champions Design as a critical driver of company goals.
- Ownership: Inspires accountability at all levels; proposes new opportunities for the company.
- Craft: Exemplifies highest standards of craftsmanship; defines industry standards in an AI-first world.
- Product Impact: Drives strategy and vision for top company goals; sets the pace for multiple teams.
- Systems Thinking: Defines systems and standards that enable cohesive experiences at scale.

### Design Management Ladder

**L6 (Design Manager)**
- Influence: Accountable for quality/execution of a product area.
- Collaboration: Amplifies team voices to shape decisions; fosters trust with cross-functional leaders.
- Ownership: Owns team learning and advancement; accountable for successes and failures.
- Craft: Accountable for quality of all team members; stays close to details and AI usage.
- Leadership: Manages recruitment; conducts career development conversations for ICs.

**L7 (Design Lead)**
- Influence: Shapes design quality standards; creates avenues for reports to influence a brand/domain.
- Collaboration: Key leadership partner; promotes automation.
- Ownership: Accountable for team's adoption of emerging tools; uncovers new business opportunities.
- Craft: Hands-on for critical projects; ensures innovation at forefront of implementation.
- Leadership: Owns hiring/retention of senior talent; adapts org structures and forecasts headcount.

**L8 (Design Lead)**
- Influence: Advances top company priorities; enables organizations to innovate and succeed.
- Collaboration: Senior leadership partner; aligns decisions across the company.
- Ownership: Inspires proactive problem-solving at all levels; accountable for overall company success.
- Craft: High-level strategy focus but stays connected to critical details.
- Leadership: Scales large, complex organizations; mentors leaders at all levels.
