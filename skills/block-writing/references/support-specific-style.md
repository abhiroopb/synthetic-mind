# Support-Specific Style Guide

Builds on Block writing principles, Cash App/Square voice, point of view, inclusive language, and style/mechanics.

## Links
- Link full titles or surface names—no generic "click here"
- Don't list URLs in full; use descriptive hyperlinked text
- Avoid multiple hyperlinked phrases in a single sentence; use bullets instead if needed

## Instruction Steps
**When to use steps**: describing a workflow, troubleshooting, or sequential instructions

**Tone**: technical, precise, instructional, and clear

**Format rules**:
- Use ordinal numbers; one action per step
- Bold UI text (buttons, menu items, fields, system messages)
- Begin with strong verbs; use exact UI element names
- Present tense; sentence punctuation (no exclamation marks)
- One level of hierarchy preferred; two levels max (for multi-surface instructions)
- More than three levels of hierarchy → consider creating a new article

✅ "Close" ❌ "Terminate" — use everyday verbs, not technical ones

## Apologies
- Don't use apology language unless a genuine mistake occurred
- Never in published/static content (macros, articles)
- Focus on solutions and encouragement, not sympathy
- ❌ "We apologize for the inconvenience" — customer problems are rarely just inconveniences
- Reserve apologies for genuine failures (longer-than-usual wait times, process errors)

## Support Team Terminology

**External-facing (customer copy)**
- Use lowercase "support" when talking about the help customers receive
- ✅ "Get support" / "Contact support" ❌ "Get Square support" / "Get Cash App support"
- When specifying the business team: "[Business name] Support" (capitalized)
  - ✅ "Square Support" / "Someone from Square Support will contact you."
  - ❌ "Square support" (lowercase) / "Someone from Support will contact you."

| Term | Tone | Best for |
|------|------|---------|
| "a support team member" | Neutral | Help Center articles, chatbot messages, structured content |
| "someone from our support team" | Personal/warm | Escalations, handoffs, conversations |
| "our team" | Casual | Informal emails, apology messages (only when context is obvious) |

**Internal-facing documentation**

| Term | Use |
|------|-----|
| Advocate | Frontline support team members; use in knowledge articles |
| Team member | When referencing permissions/roles in Square Dashboard |
| Support team | Block Customer Operations organization broadly |
| Customer | External app/product user (not "user") |
| Seller | Square business users (in Square-facing internal docs) |

- Spell out full team names on first reference, then acronym (title case)
- ✅ "Transfer to the Cash Escalation Support (CES) team" ❌ "Transfer to CES"
- ✅ "Contact the Policy Governance team" ❌ "Escalate to compliance"
