# Email and Voice Patterns

## Transactional Email Patterns

### Subject Lines
Clear, specific, actionable.

**Examples**:
- "You sent $50 to Alex"
- "Your Cash App Card is on the way"
- "Verify your email address"
- "Your statement is ready"

**Avoid**:
- "Transaction notification" (too generic)
- "Important message from Cash App" (not specific)

### Email Body Structure
1. Clear, specific subject/heading
2. Key details (amount, recipient, date)
3. Next steps or related actions (if applicable)
4. Link to relevant page (if applicable)

## Voice Adaptation by Content Type

### High-Stakes Content
Account security, large transactions, legal matters.

**Tone**: Serious, transparent, reassuring
**Example**: "Your account has been temporarily locked to protect your money. Here's what to do next."

### Routine Content
Small transactions, balance checks, settings.

**Tone**: Efficient, direct, minimal explanation
**Example**: "Balance: $247.89"

### First-Time Content
Onboarding, new features, setup flows.

**Tone**: Patient, explanatory, encouraging
**Example**: "Let's set up direct deposit. We'll guide you through each step."

### Error Content
Something went wrong.

**Tone**: Empathetic, solution-focused, never blame users
**Example**: "We couldn't process your payment. Your card may have expired. Try a different card or update your payment info."

### Success Content
Confirmations, completions.

**Tone**: Positive, specific, proportional
**Example**: "Money sent to Alex" (routine success, brief)
**Example**: "Your account is all set up. You're ready to send and receive money." (major success, slightly more detail)
