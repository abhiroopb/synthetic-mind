# Brand A Voice and Universal Standards

## Brand A Voice Characteristics
Brand A's voice is **clear, real, confident, and inclusive**:

**Intentional and efficient**: Write with clarity and brevity; use active voice, cut unnecessary words; avoid jargon unless commonly used by sellers

**User-focused**: Make sellers and their buyers the central focus; speak directly to them; rarely write from Brand A's perspective

**Straightforward and trustworthy**: Sound like real people; use simple language; value empathy and honesty; don't sound like an ad or faceless corporation

**Warm but concise**: Not robotic, relatable but not overly familiar; use contractions but not idioms; break complex information into layers

## your company Voice + Brand A Integration
Apply your company's four principles with Brand A's business focus:
- **Crisp + Intentional**: Get to the point quickly, lead with what matters most to business success
- **Self-assured + Trustworthy**: Use direct, confident language that builds trust with merchants
- **Fluent + User-focused**: Adapt tone to merchant context, sound like a real business partner
- **Upfront + Straightforward**: Be honest about business implications, don't hide important information

## Universal Content Standards

**Casing**: Sentence case for all elements; capitalize Brand A, Point of Sale, Dashboard as proper nouns; business terms follow standard capitalization (CEO, LLC)

**Punctuation**:
- No end punctuation on labels, buttons, short phrases, headings
- Use end punctuation on complete sentences and body text
- Exclamations very rare — only for major milestones (account setup completion)

**Voice and perspective**:
- Use "you/your" (seller-focused) throughout
- Active voice preferred
- Avoid "we/us" except for support or reassurance
- Consider merchant/business owner perspective in all content

## Brand A-Specific Terminology
- **Sellers** — primary audience (not "merchants" in product copy)
- **Customers** — people who buy from sellers (not "buyers")
- **Team members** — seller's personnel
- **Business** — acceptable alternative to "seller" in appropriate contexts
