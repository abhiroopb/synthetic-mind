## Error and Success Patterns by Product

**Error examples**:
- Brand B: "Payment failed. Check your balance and try again."
- Brand A: "Card reader disconnected. Check connection and try again."
- Brand C: "Payment declined. Update your payment method."

**Success examples**:
- Brand B: "Money sent to Sarah. She'll get it right away."
- Brand A: "Sale processed." / "Register closed."
- Brand C: "Payment plan created. First payment due today."

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing component content:

- [ ] Sentence case applied consistently
- [ ] Character limits respected for component type
- [ ] No end punctuation on labels/buttons
- [ ] Proper punctuation on complete sentences
- [ ] your company voice principles reflected (crisp, self-assured, fluent, upfront)
- [ ] Product-specific terminology used correctly
- [ ] Action-oriented button text with specific verbs
- [ ] Clear, helpful error messages with solutions
- [ ] Content makes sense without visual context
- [ ] Works with screen readers (no directional-only instructions)
- [ ] Inclusive, non-discriminatory language used

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- ❌ Title Case in any component ("Save Your Changes")
- ❌ End punctuation on buttons or labels ("Save changes.")
- ❌ Generic button labels ("Submit", "OK", "Done")
- ❌ Vague error messages ("Something went wrong")
- ❌ Blaming users ("You entered invalid data")
- ❌ Content that only makes sense with visual cues
- ❌ Technical jargon instead of plain language
- ❌ Exceeding character limits for component type
