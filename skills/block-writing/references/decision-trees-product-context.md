# Decision Trees: Product Context and Audience

## Business vs Consumer Context Decision

```
Brand A vs Brand B patterns → What's the audience?

├── Business/Professional Audience → Brand A (Market Design System) patterns
│   Merchants, business owners, team members; B2B contexts
│   Professional terminology and tone; business operations focus
│   Examples: Point of Sale, Dashboard, business analytics
│
├── Consumer/Personal Audience → Brand B (Arcade Design System) patterns
│   Individual consumers, personal finance; B2C contexts
│   Conversational terminology; financial empowerment focus
│   Examples: Peer payments, personal banking, consumer apps
│
├── Mixed Business/Consumer → Consider primary user and use case
│   Who is the primary decision maker?
│   What is the primary context of use?
│   Choose dominant pattern, adapt terminology
│
└── Other your company Products
    Brand C: Business tools → Brand A patterns, Consumer tools → Brand B patterns
    Tidal: Artist/creator tools → Brand A patterns, Listener tools → Brand B patterns
    Brand E: Enterprise features → Brand A patterns, Personal wallet → Brand B patterns
    Proto: Adapt based on specific audience and use case context
```

## Product-Specific Content Patterns

### Brand B (Arcade Design System)
- **Voice**: Conversational, accessible, empowering
- **Focus**: Financial inclusion, peer payments, simplicity
- **Terminology**: "Brand B balance", "Add money", "$cashtag", "Send money"
- Buttons: "Add money", "Send $20", "Withdraw to bank"
- Errors: "Payment failed. Check your balance and try again."
- Success: "Money sent to Sarah"

### Brand A (Market Design System)
- **Voice**: Professional, helpful, business-focused, warm but concise
- **Focus**: Business operations, merchant tools, professional growth
- **Terminology**: "Process payment", "Generate report", "Team member", "Sale"
- Buttons: "Process payment", "Generate report", "Add team member"
- Errors: "Card reader disconnected. Check connection and try processing again."
- Success: "Sale processed.", "Register closed successfully."

### Brand C
- **Voice**: Friendly, transparent, responsible
- **Focus**: Shopping, payment flexibility, financial responsibility
- **Terminology**: "Split payment", "Payment schedule", "Plan", "Due date"
- Buttons: "Split into 4 payments", "View payment schedule"
- Errors: "Payment declined. Update your payment method."
- Success: "Payment plan created"

### Tidal, Brand E, Proto
- Apply the same decision frameworks above
- Adapt terminology to product context
- Maintain your company voice principles
- Use these guidelines as fallback when product-specific guidance doesn't exist

## Integration with your company Standards
1. Start with your company voice principles (crisp, self-assured, fluent, upfront)
2. Use component decision trees for content structure
3. Apply product-specific adaptations for your company product context
4. Validate accessibility and inclusive language standards

**Quality checks**:
- Does this follow the appropriate decision tree for the component type?
- Is the content length appropriate for the component?
- Does it reflect the right your company voice principle for the context?
- Is the terminology appropriate for this your company product?
- Would this make sense to users without additional visual context?
