# Marketing Inclusivity and Transparency

## Race and Ethnicity
Always default to a person's preferred self-description—ask. Only mention race/ethnicity when relevant (biographical milestones, demonstrations involving race, detailed suspect descriptions).

- **BIPOC** preferred over "minority" (avoid "minority"—othering)
- **Asian vs. Asian-American**: distinguish; specify when possible (Indian-American, Taiwanese-American)
- ❌ "Oriental"
- **Black** (not "African-American") unless context or personal preference calls for it; avoid "black/white" as nouns (OK in statistical breakdowns); capitalize Black
- **Latino**: Latin-American origin; **Hispanic**: descendants from Spain (use specific when possible—Puerto Rican, etc.); **Latinx**: gender-neutral alternative for those who don't identify as male or female

## Pronouns, Sex, and Gender
Use **LGBTQ+** for the whole community.

Ask "What are your pronouns?" — not "preferred pronouns" (implies a choice)

**Transgender guidelines**
- Adjective only—never a noun; always use chosen name (avoid deadnaming)
- Transgender women ≠ "cross-dressers" or drag queens
- Use "transition" instead of "pre-operative" or "post-operative"
- ❌ cross-dressing, cross-dresser, she-male, he-she, trannie, tranny, gender bender, sex change operation, transvestite, pre-operative, post-operative

Refer to GLAAD's media reference guide for authoritative transgender language guidance.

## Abilities
- **Deaf/deaf**: uppercase "D" for the Deaf community/culture; lowercase "d" for audiological status
- Preferred terms: "Deaf" or "hard of hearing" — avoid "hearing impaired" unless self-identified
- Follow NCDJ (National Center on Disability and Journalism) guidance

## Transparency
Be honest about what products can and can't do. Don't overpromise. When writing about financial products:
- Avoid absolutes: "guaranteed", "always", "never", "foolproof"
- Don't imply guaranteed financial outcomes: "grow your money", "get rich"
- Specify limitations: "up to $200", "at select merchants", "available where Visa is accepted"
- Be upfront about fees, terms, and eligibility requirements
