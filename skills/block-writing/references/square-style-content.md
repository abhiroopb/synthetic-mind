# Square Style: Content Guidelines

## Readability
- Paragraphs: max 6 lines/100 words on desktop; aim for 5 lines/70 words; shorter for mobile
- Headings: should convey main points so scanning headers gives a summary
- Lists: use bullets when listing 3+ items that are more than a couple words each

## Competitors
Don't call out or undermine competitors. Focus on Square's value to sellers.
- **Respectful**: no disparaging or mocking language
- **Seller-first**: frame benefits as what sellers gain
- **Fact-based**: direct comparisons must be accurate and verifiable
- **Outcome-focused**: highlight seller results, not competitor weaknesses

✅ "Square has no long-term contracts or hidden fees — you only pay for what you use."
❌ "Unlike Toast or Clover, Square doesn't lock you in with misleading contracts."

## Humor
Use sparingly and intentionally—only where it fits naturally (social posts, brand campaigns, light marketing).
- Seller-centered: comes from real business challenges, not at a seller's expense
- Respectful: never when stakes are high or trust is on the line

**Where humor is NOT appropriate:**
- Banking, lending, money-movement communications
- Support articles, troubleshooting, error messages
- Legal, regulatory, or compliance content
- Formal announcements (pricing updates, policy changes)

## Quotes and Testimonials
All quotes on square.com must be legitimate and attributed to real, existing people with their permission.

**Attribution format**: Name of Seller, title (lowercase) / Business Name / Location / [optional] Vertical
- ✅ "co-owner", "owner", "chef and owner" ❌ "Co-Owner", "Owner" (title case)
- Ask for preferred title (as important as asking for pronouns)
- Spell out state name when space allows for warmth

## In-App vs. Out-of-App Copy
- **In-app**: keep as short as possible; get to the point immediately
- **Out-of-app** (blog, email): more room to play; use information hierarchy; subheads should summarize sections

Always prioritize: clear, unhindered, informative, actionable language.

## In-Product Wayfinding
- **Bold UI elements**: always bold button/tab/menu names in instructions
- Use arrows (>) for longer navigation paths; step-by-step language for shorter paths
- ✅ "Go to **More** > **Settings** > **Hardware** > **Connect and Manage**"
- ✅ "Tap **More**, then tap **Balance**"
- Use exact label as it appears in product—no paraphrasing

## Titling and Formatting
**Headlines (advertising)**: sentence case for most; need a clear, complete message
- ✅ "Set your team up for success" ❌ "Tools for teams" (too vague)

**Blog post / article titles**: Title Case (capitalize words with 4+ letters, except prepositions)
- ✅ "Cat in Suspenders Steals 15 Sack Lunches"
- No period at end of title unless multiple sentences

**Everything else** (social ads, landing page headlines, email subject lines): sentence case
- ✅ "Sign up now for a $500 bonus" ❌ "Sign Up Now for a $500 Bonus"

**YouTube titles**: Title Case (per YouTube best practice)

## Disclaimers
**Running text**: superscript numeral after punctuation; single space before next sentence
- ✅ "This is an example.¹ Next sentence." ❌ "example¹."

**Footnotes**: superscript numeral at start; no space between numeral and text; no italics
- ✅ "¹This is an example." ❌ "¹ This is an example."

**Demand Gen/Marketo**: use asterisk (*) for forms; place Privacy Policy disclaimer after email input

**Metadata format**: Product Description in Title Case | Product Name
