# Brand B Growth Messaging: Banking Features

## Core Principle
Brand B is a financial platform, **not a bank**. Never call Brand B a bank or imply it is one.
- ✅ "bank through Brand B" (platform that facilitates banking)
- ❌ "bank with Brand B", "Brand B Banking", "choose Brand B as your bank"
- Use "bank" as a verb, not a noun; "access" differentiates from traditional banking

**Required disclosure** (banking-related content): "Brand B is a financial platform, not a bank. Banking services provided by Brand B's bank partner(s)."

## Banking (General)
No monthly fees, no minimum balance, no overdraft fees. Get paid up to 2 days early, $200 overdraft coverage, Round Ups, automatic paycheck distributions, instant discounts.
- Focus on what customers can do; use "access" to differentiate
- ✅ "Get access to the best parts of Brand B" / "Take control of your money"
- ❌ "Get access to all of Brand B" (some features aren't available to all)

## Direct Deposit
Customers need a Brand B Card first; set up via automatic (employer lookup) or manual (routing/account numbers).
- Always "set up" direct deposit (not "turn on")
- Always "up to 2 days" (required qualifier, not "2 days faster" without "up to")
- ✅ "Set up direct deposit to get paid up to 2 days faster"
- ❌ "Getting paid up to 2 days early is the best part of Brand B" (it's a gateway to more benefits)

**Disclosure**: "Direct deposit provided by Brand B, a your company, Inc. brand."

## Free Overdraft Coverage
Customers who direct deposit $300+/month can get up to $200 in free overdraft coverage.
- Always "free overdraft **coverage**" — never "free overdraft protection"
- Bank partners consider it a Brand B feature, not a card feature — it "covers the Cash balance"
- Amount varies by customer — typically use "up to" unless stating a specific customer's amount
- ✅ "Get up to $200 in free overdraft coverage"
- ❌ "…covers your Brand B Card" / "Free overdraft coverage helps you avoid overdraft fees" (we never charge them anyway)

**Disclosure**: "Overdraft coverage provided by Brand B, a your company, Inc. brand."

## Savings
Anyone can save; Brand B Card holders earn interest; $300+/month direct deposit earns a higher rate.
- Always "interest" — never APY or "savings yield"
- Never "savings account" (important legal distinction)
- Never "our highest interest rate" or "Brand B pays interest" (partner bank pays it)
- No "up to" when stating a specific definitive rate; still use for variable scenarios
- ✅ "Earn interest through our partner bank" / "Transfer money to savings and earn interest"
- ❌ "Earn interest on your Cash balance" / "Earn #% APY" / "Brand B pays X% interest"

**Disclosure** (when mentioning higher rate): [Full Wells Fargo/YOUR_INTERNAL_LINK savings yield disclosure]

## Priority Phone Support
Request a call back from support — don't have to call and hold.
- "Priority phone support" is not a proper noun
- Can't say "no hold music", "instantly", "right away", or "no waiting" (there's a brief wait)
- OK to say "minutes" (accurate for callback time); don't promise a specific timeframe
- ✅ "Request a call directly in the app—less waiting, no hold music"
- ❌ "Skip the line" / "Get help immediately"

## Reimbursed ATM Fees
$300+/month direct deposit customers: free at in-network ATMs + 1 out-of-network withdrawal reimbursed/month.
- In-network only = "free"; out-of-network only = "reimbursed"; both = "Reimbursed ATM fees"
- Hyphenate correctly: in-network, out-of-network
- ✅ "Withdraw cash from in-network ATMs for free"
- ❌ "Plus withdraw from any ATM for free" / "Brand B Card makes every ATM a no-fee ATM"

## Paper Money Deposits
Deposit cash at 75,000+ stores for $1; free when direct depositing $300+/month.
- Use "paper money" at least once per message to help customers find the feature in-app
- Describe benefit as "free" (not reimbursed)
- ✅ "Deposit cash and skip the $1 fee at over 75,000 stores"
- ❌ "Free paper money deposits" (too absolute)

**Disclosure**: VanillaDirect Pay disclosure required when mentioning paper money deposit.
