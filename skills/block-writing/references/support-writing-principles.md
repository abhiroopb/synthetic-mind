# Support Writing Principles

Effective support content empowers customers and internal teams to take action confidently, efficiently, and independently.

## One article, one purpose
- Each article addresses a single task, issue, or decision
- Avoid mixing unrelated topics or multiple paths (unless variations of the same process)

## Be clear, concise, and purposeful
- Plain language; get to the point quickly
- 6th-8th grade reading level
- Active voice; include only the context needed to act

## Structure to guide and empower
- Start with key context: who it's for, what it's about, any setup needed
- Troubleshooting: organize steps least to most invasive
- Use headings, bullets/numbered lists, and accordions for scanning
- Accordions: use for two+ surfaces (e.g., web vs. app) or for multi-level options

## Write for the customer first
- Start with customer intent — not internal process or policy
- Keep sentences short, instructions direct, paragraphs scannable
- Design for independence; help customers succeed on their own
- Avoid creating dependency on further contact or escalation when self-serve is possible

## Global audience
- Write for localization from the start — avoid idioms, metaphors, and region-specific terms
- Use consistent terminology from Cash App and Square glossaries

## Consistent across channels
- Same voice and tone across knowledge articles, quick texts/macros, and internal content
- Consistent naming for surfaces, apps, and features
