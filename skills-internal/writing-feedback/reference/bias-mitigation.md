# Bias Mitigation for Performance Management

## Why This Matters
Performance evaluations impact promotions, compensation, and visibility. Even well-intended managers can let unconscious bias skew decisions, leading to inequities that compound over time.

## Common Biases to Watch For

| Bias Type | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Proximity Bias** | Favoring those you see or interact with most frequently. |
| **Affinity Bias** | Preferring those who remind you of yourself. |
| **Halo/Horns Effect** | Letting one trait (good or bad) shape your whole evaluation. |
| **Recency Bias** | Overemphasizing the most recent events. |
| **Confirmation Bias** | Only noticing behaviors that support your initial opinion. |

## Inclusive Evaluation Practices

1. **Anchor in Expectations:** Use role clarity, scope, and success criteria (not gut feel) as your foundation.
2. **Review the Full Performance Period:** Look at notes, feedback, and projects from the full review window, not just the last few weeks.
3. **Audit for Language:** Watch for vague or coded terms (e.g., "not a leader," "quiet," "aggressive"). Be clear and specific.
4. **Engage in Calibration Thoughtfully:** Ask: "Who's being consistently elevated in this process? Who's missing from the conversation?"

## Reflection Prompts for Managers

- Am I evaluating behavior or relying on perception?
- Have I supported all team members equally with feedback and visibility?
- Who received stretch opportunities and why?

## Lead Best Practices

- Reflect before rating to avoid rushing.
- Review evaluation language with peers.
- Audit who's getting credit and who's being overlooked.
- Compare feedback and ratings across your team.
- Ask: "Would I say this about someone else in a similar situation?"
- Draft evaluations early to allow for review and reflection.
- Join calibration sessions prepared with examples and data.

## Language Red Flags

Avoid these vague or coded patterns:
- "Not a culture fit" (specify what behaviors are misaligned)
- "Quiet" or "reserved" (describe specific communication gaps if relevant)
- "Aggressive" or "abrasive" (describe the specific behavior and its impact)
- "Natural leader" (describe what leadership behaviors were demonstrated)
- "Potential" without evidence (anchor in what was actually delivered)

Replace with clear, observable, fact-based descriptions of actions and their outcomes.
