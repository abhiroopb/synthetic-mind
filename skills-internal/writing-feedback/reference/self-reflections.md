# Guide to Self-Reflections

## Purpose
Self-reflection is a continuous, lightweight tool for personal growth, visibility, and alignment at your company. It is not a formal self-evaluation. It provides managers with context, clarity, and insight to support ongoing development and performance.

## Why Reflecting Matters
- **Clarity:** Make sense of your progress and how you're showing up.
- **Visibility:** Ensure your manager has insight into your contributions.
- **Growth:** Learn from your experiences.
- **Alignment:** Stay connected to your role, its objectives, and development goals.
- **Readiness:** Prepare naturally for 1:1s, check-ins, and career conversations.
- **Self-advocacy:** Tell your own story as it unfolds.

## When to Reflect
- At the beginning of a performance window
- After a project wraps
- At the end of a quarter
- After completing advanced learning
- During 1:1s or growth conversations
- When you feel proud, stretched, or learned something new

## What to Include
1. **What you achieved:** Highlight accomplishments, progress, or milestones.
2. **How you worked to achieve it:** Describe your behaviors, collaboration, and approach.
3. **The impact or results it had:** Share outcomes, ripple effects, or value created.
4. **What you learned:** Capture key takeaways, growth moments, or new skills.

## How to Draft Using IBB

### Impact
| What outcomes did I achieve? | Prompts |
|-----|---------|
| How your work delivered value for your team, customers, or the company overall. | "I created value by..." / "A challenge I navigated was..." / "One thing I'm proud of this week/month is..." |

### Behaviors
| How did I show up and collaborate? | Prompts |
|-----|---------|
| How you work with others, day-to-day actions, communication, and inclusion. Ties to level-specific expectations in career ladders. | "A behavior I'm working on is... and here's how I've practiced it..." / "Feedback I received and acted on was..." / "I noticed that I've improved in..." |

### Betterment
| How did I grow, improve, or innovate? | Prompts |
|-----|---------|
| Personal development and continuous improvement for yourself, your team, or the way we work. | "One learning or insight I had was..." / "Something I'll try next time is..." / "I've made progress on my growth goals by..." |

## SBI Framework (Complementary)
The Situation-Behavior-Impact model helps structure reflections with specificity:
- **Situation:** Describe the context or moment.
- **Behavior:** Share the observable actions you took.
- **Impact:** Explain the result, what changed because of your action.

## Examples of Strong Reflections

**Strength-oriented (focus on impact):**
"Over the past quarter, I led the rollout of the new merchant onboarding flow, which decreased time-to-first-transaction by 18%. I partnered with Design to simplify the UI and worked closely with Support to train our frontline teams. A big growth moment was navigating a conflict between teams, I used feedback from a previous retro to build alignment early. I'm proud of how I applied those lessons to improve cross-functional collaboration."

**Growth-opportunity (focus on behavior):**
"I've been focusing on clearer communication, especially in team updates. Last month, I received feedback that my summaries helped unblock downstream teams faster. I've started using a new async format that seems to resonate. I'm continuing to look for ways to communicate with more clarity and context."

**Focus on betterment:**
"I noticed that engagement in our local community group had dropped off since the start of the year. I organized a listening session to gather input, then collaborated with other members to revamp our monthly events with more inclusive and relevant themes. Attendance and participation have steadily improved, and a few new members have stepped into planning roles. This felt like a step toward contributing at the next level by strengthening our culture and fostering a more connected, inclusive community."

## Tips
- Start small: Even short reflections count.
- Stay consistent: Build a story over time.
- Keep it authentic: Your voice matters more than polish.
- Reflect often, not just at review time.
- Share reflections with your manager in 1:1s.
