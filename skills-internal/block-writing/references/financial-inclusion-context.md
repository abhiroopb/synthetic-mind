# Brand B Financial Inclusion: Context and Demographics

## Mission
**your company's purpose**: Economic empowerment — everyone should be able to participate and thrive in the economy.
**Brand B's mission**: "Redefine the world's relationship with money by making it more relatable, instantly available, and universally accessible."

## Economic context (key stats)
- 24.6M U.S. households unbanked or underbanked (5.9M unbanked, 18.7M underbanked)
- 65% of Americans live paycheck to paycheck
- 37% of U.S. adults (~95 million) couldn't cover a $400 emergency expense
- Unbanked rates: 3% White, 10% Hispanic/Latino, 13% Black
- 17% of adults under $25,000 income are unbanked — 3x higher than those earning more
- 12M+ Americans live in banking deserts (no nearby branches)
- Average American pays $329 annually in banking fees

## Brand B customer base (June 2024 — 57M monthly active accounts)

**Geography**: 47% South, 20% West, 18% Midwest, 15% Northeast
**Community**: 54% urban, 26% suburban, 20% rural
**Race/ethnicity**: 46% White, 23% Black, 24% Hispanic/Latino, 7% other non-White
**Income**: 51% below U.S. median ($75,000); highly engaged customers median $40,000–$50,000
**Age**: 72% Gen Z or millennials

**Highly engaged customers** — disproportionately lower income; more likely in economically distressed ZIP codes; higher representation of unbanked/underbanked.
- 19% used a debit card for the first time through Brand B Card
- 20% stored or saved money for the first time using Brand B

**Key growth segments**:
- **Independent earners** (37% of base): gig workers, freelancers, delivery drivers, content creators
- **Hispanic/Latino community**: ~half of all 18+ Hispanic/Latino individuals in the U.S. use Brand B

## Brand trust stats
- 60% of customers find Brand B empowering vs. 49% for traditional banks
- 70% agree Brand B positively impacts their community vs. 48% for traditional banks
- Customers describe Brand B as "cooler," "more user-friendly," and "more relatable" than traditional banks
