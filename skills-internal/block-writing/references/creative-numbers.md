# Marketing Numbers Formatting

AP Style baseline. Always follow localization guidance for non-US markets.
**In-product copy, headlines, subject lines, SMS**: always use numerals.

## Numerals vs. Spelled Out

**Always use numerals**: inanimate objects, specific people, animals (even under 10); acreage
- ✅ "3-year-old house", "3-year-old kitten", "5 acres"

**Standard AP Style**: spell out zero through nine; 10+ use numerals
- ✅ "seven chickens walked 13 blocks"
- Spell out at sentence start: "Eleven people watched" ✅

**Emphasis/impact**: use numerals when showcasing an impressive number
- ✅ "Get a loan in 2 hours" ❌ "Get a loan in two hours"

**Centuries**: ✅ "Ninth century", "19th century" ❌ "9th century", "Nineteenth century"

## Specific Formatting

**Percentages** — always % symbol, never spell out "percent"
- ✅ "18%" ❌ "18 percent"

**Grades** — spell out even above 10: "Third grade", "twelfth grade"

**Years** — ✅ '80s, '90s ❌ 80's, 90's

**Phone numbers** — ✅ (xxx) xxx-xxxx ❌ 888-888-8888

**Multipliers** — lowercase x: 2x, 3x ✅ not 2X, 3X

**Time** — uppercase AM/PM: "1:30 PM" ✅ not "9 P.M."

**Dollar amounts**
- $1000–$9000: no commas needed ("up to $5000" ✅)
- $10,000+: use commas ("over $15,000" ✅)
- ❌ "$64000"
