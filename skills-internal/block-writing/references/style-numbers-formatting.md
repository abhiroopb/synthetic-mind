# Style: Numbers and Formatting

## Numbers — Use Numerals
Use numerals instead of spelling out numbers.

- ✅ "You have 3 pending payments" → ❌ "You have three pending payments"
- ✅ "Wait 2 minutes" → ❌ "Wait two minutes"

**Exception**: When starting a sentence, rework it instead.
- Better: "You have 2 pending payments" (not "Two payments are pending")

## Number Formatting

**Comma separators**: Use for 4+ digits
- ✅ $1,000 / $10,000 / 1,234 people
- No comma for 3 digits: $999, 100 people

**Currency**: No space between symbol and number
- ✅ $42.89 / €50.00
- ❌ $ 42.89 / € 50.00

**Decimals**: Use for cents/precision
- ✅ $42.89 / $42.00 (include .00 for precise amounts)
- OK: $42 (acceptable for round numbers in some contexts)

## Dates and Times

**Dates** — spell out when space allows:
- ✅ "January 15, 2026" (preferred)
- ✅ "Jan 15, 2026" (limited space)
- ✅ "1/15/26" (very limited space only)

**Times**:
- ✅ "2:30 PM" (space before AM/PM)
- ✅ "2 PM" (no minutes if on the hour)
- ❌ "2:30PM" (no space)

**Time ranges**:
- ✅ "2–4 PM" (en dash, AM/PM only after last time)
- ✅ "2:30–4:30 PM"

## Phone Numbers
Use standard US formatting with hyphens or parentheses.

- ✅ (555) 123-4567 or 555-123-4567
- ❌ 5551234567 (hard to read)

## Abbreviations

**Common abbreviations — no periods needed**:
- ATM, PIN, ID, US (not A.T.M., P.I.N., I.D., U.S.)

**States**: Use two-letter postal codes
- CA, NY, TX (not Calif., N.Y., Texas)

## Text Formatting

**Lists** — use bullets for easier scanning:
- Keep items parallel in structure
- Start each item with a capital letter
- Use periods only if items are complete sentences

**Bold**: For important terms or to draw attention — don't overuse

**Italics**: Rarely needed in UI copy (sometimes for book titles, foreign words)

**ALL CAPS**: Avoid in body copy — OK for acronyms (ATM, PIN), never for emphasis
