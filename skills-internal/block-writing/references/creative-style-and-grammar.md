# Marketing Style and Grammar

AP Style baseline. Always follow localization guidance for non-US markets.

## Punctuation

**Oxford comma** — use it: "planes, trains, and cars" ✅ (most non-US markets don't use it)

**Em/en dashes**
- En-dash (-): ranges and compound adjectives
- Em-dash (—): break up a phrase; include spaces around it
- ✅ "pizza — but I chose salad" ❌ "pizza--but" or no spaces

**Exclamation points** — avoid; only when genuinely needed

**Asterisks** — rare; place outside end punctuation (e.g., Free kittens!*); use superscripts for legal disclaimers

**Ampersands** — sparingly to save space or for combined words (R&B, Q&A — no spaces around)

**Footnotes/superscripts** — numerical, starting with 1
- Running text: superscript after punctuation — `example.¹ Next sentence.` ✅ not `example¹.`
- Footnote: `¹This is an example.` ✅ not `¹ This is an example.` (no space)

**Acronyms**
- Spell out on first use with parenthetical: "Work From Home (WFH)" ✅
- No periods in acronyms: "US" ✅ not "U.S."
- Exception: APR/APY don't need spelling out

## Spacing
Never double space after a period.

## Capitalization
- Capitalize proper names of nationalities, peoples, races, tribes
- **Stat + descriptor pairs**: sentence case — "15M+ appointments booked monthly" ✅ not "15M+ Appointments Booked Monthly"
