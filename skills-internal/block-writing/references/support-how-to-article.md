# Square How-To Article Template

## Principles
- One article, one purpose — ~650 words or less
- Headings must make article scannable
- Be explicit about which surface (Dashboard, POS app) each step uses

## Article structure
Title → Who is this for → About [feature] → Before you begin → Step/Option 1 → Step/Option 2... → Related articles

## Writing each section

**Title** — verb phrase, sentence case; use "invisible how to" (reads as "how to [task]")
- Add vertical product name when needed: "Schedule your team with Square for Restaurants"

**Who is this for** — callout box with heading; covers:
- Permissions: "Account owners or team members with the [category] permission to [specific permission]. Set permissions in Square Dashboard."
- Subscriptions: legacy (Plus/Premium/Free) then P&P tiers (Square Free/Plus/Premium — US only)
- POS modes (lowercase): full service, quick service, bar, retail, bookings, services, standard

**About [feature]** — 1-2 sentences; present tense; no hyperlinks; copy from sibling article if exists

**Before you begin** — bullet list, present tense:
- Surfaces: "You have # options to complete [task]:" or "You can [task] from your [surface(s)]."
- Hardware: "You also need [hardware]. Learn how to [article]."
- Prior setup: "You also need to already have [feature] set up. Learn how to [article]."
- Location/timing: "To [task], you need to be [location]." / "Avoid [task] during [time] because [effect]."

**Steps** — ordinal numbers, present tense, bold UI text, verb-first
- Dashboard: "Sign in to Square Dashboard and go to [path] > [final destination]." — link to final destination
- App: "Open the [POS app] and tap/select…" — list SuperPOS modes first (full service, quick service, bar, retail, bookings, services, standard), then legacy apps
- For paths at Items & services or Orders & payments L1: add vertical names in parentheses
- If all modes use same steps: use "Point of sale app" as accordion header, omit mode names

## Hyperlinks
- Link text = full article title; preface "Learn how to [article]" for support links
- Dashboard links: preface "Sign in" and link to final destination in the path
- No "click here," "here," or "learn more" link text
- Limit to instruction items only (not intro or feature sections)

## Callouts — use sparingly (secondary notification, no heading)
- Blocking actions
- Contact support needed (after all self-serve steps exhausted)
- Downstream effects (payments, data, timing delays)

## Related articles
H2 "Related articles" — bulleted list, full title hyperlinked, max 5; same subtopic or prerequisite/follow-on relationship
