# Style: Punctuation

## Oxford Commas
Always use Oxford commas (comma before "and" in a list of three or more items).

- ✅ "Cash App, Square, and Afterpay"
- ❌ "Cash App, Square and Afterpay"
- ✅ "Send, receive, and invest money"

## Em Dashes
No spaces around em dashes.

- ✅ "Your payment is processing—it'll arrive soon"
- ❌ "Your payment is processing — it'll arrive soon"

## Periods

**Use periods**:
- In body copy and full sentences
- In helper text and descriptions
- In longer notifications

**Don't use periods**:
- In button labels
- In navigation items
- In form field labels
- In short status messages
- In headings

**Examples**:
- ✅ Button: "Save changes" (no period)
- ✅ Helper text: "We'll send a code to verify your phone number." (period)
- ✅ Heading: "Connect your bank account" (no period)
- ✅ Body text: "Your payment is on the way. It'll arrive in 1-3 business days." (periods)

## Exclamation Marks
Use sparingly and only one at a time.

**When to use**: Genuine celebrations or significant achievements
**When not to use**: Routine confirmations, error messages, professional/business contexts

- ✅ "You got paid!" (significant incoming payment)
- ❌ "Transaction complete!" (routine, don't over-celebrate)
- ❌ "Error occurred!" (never in error messages)
- ❌ "Click here!!!" (multiple marks)

## Question Marks
Use only when asking a direct question. Never use multiple question marks.

- ✅ "Need help?"
- ✅ "Where should we send the money?"
- ❌ "Need help??" (multiple marks)
- ❌ "Transaction failed?" (not a question, it's a statement)

## Semicolons
Avoid in conversational copy. Use periods or em dashes instead.

- ✅ "Your payment failed. Try another card."
- ❌ "Your payment failed; try another card."

## Colons
Use sparingly.

- ✅ "Total: $42.89" (acceptable for labels)
- ✅ "Total $42.89" (also acceptable, more conversational)
