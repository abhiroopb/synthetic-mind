# Product Voice: Brand A

## Voice Characteristics
- **Professional**: Business-appropriate without being stiff
- **Helpful**: Supportive, like a knowledgeable colleague
- **Business-focused**: Emphasize tools that help businesses grow

## Target Audience
Merchants and business owners; sellers (individuals to enterprises); business operations focus; varying technical sophistication

## Tone by Context

**Business operations** (sales, inventory, staff): Professional and efficient; more formal than Brand B but still conversational
- "Track inventory across locations"

**Financial tools** (reports, analytics): Clear and informative; data-focused
- "Your top-selling items this month"

**Getting started** (setup, onboarding): Supportive and thorough; more detail than Brand B
- "Let's set up your first product. Add a name, price, and photo."

**Errors**: Professional but empathetic; business impact and solutions
- "This payment couldn't be processed. The customer's card was declined. Ask them to try another payment method."

## Product-Specific Terminology

**Audience terms**:
- ✅ "sellers" (preferred), "merchants", "business owners", "customers" (people who buy)
- ❌ "users" (too generic in business context)

**Business operations**:
- "items" or "products" (not "SKUs" in most contexts)
- "staff" or "team members" (not "employees" unless specifically about employment)
- "locations" (not "stores" unless specifically retail)

**Product names** (always capitalize correctly):
- Brand A, Brand A Point of Sale, Brand A for Retail, Brand A Online, Brand A Payroll, Hardware Terminal, Hardware Reader

## Example Patterns

**Buttons**: "Create invoice", "Add item", "Process payment", "View report", "Manage team"

**Confirmations**: "Invoice sent to customer", "Item added to inventory", "Payment processed: $127.50"

**Errors**:
- "This payment couldn't be processed. The customer's card was declined. Ask them to try another payment method."
- "We couldn't send this invoice. Check the email address and try again."

**Empty states**: "No items in your inventory yet. Add your first product to start selling."

## Consumer vs. Business Context
- Brand B (consumer): "You sent $50", "Add money", casual personal tone
- Brand A (business): "Payment received: $50.00", "Add funds to balance", professional tone
