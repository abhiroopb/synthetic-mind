# Point of View in Product Copy

## Second Person (You/Your) — Default
Speak directly to users using "you" and "your" in descriptions and explanations.
- ✅ "Go to your profile to turn on push notifications"
- ❌ "Go to My Profile to turn on push notifications"

Avoid pronouns in headers, buttons, and CTAs unless they improve clarity.
- ✅ "Change password" (button) ❌ "Change your password"

## Gender-Neutral Pronouns (They/Them/Their)
Use gender-neutral pronouns for unknown subjects.
- ✅ "When a customer recommends your product, an update appears on their profile."
- ❌ "…on his/her profile."

## First Person Singular (I/My) — Legal Consent Only
Use "I" and "my" only when obtaining consent or permission in legal contexts.
- ✅ "I agree to the terms and conditions."
- ❌ "You agree to the terms and conditions." (in consent flows)

## First Person Plural (We/Us/Our) — Support and Reassurance
Use "we/our" for:
- Support and contact: "Contact our Support team"
- Reassurance and apologies: "We couldn't verify your ID"
- Requests: "Leave a review to let us know what you think"
- Avoiding passive voice: "We'll send you a verification code to continue" ✅ (not "You'll receive a verification code")
