# Style: Voice, Tone, and Point of View

## Active Voice (Preferred)
Use active voice whenever possible for clarity and directness.

- ✅ "We sent your payment" (active)
- ❌ "Your payment was sent" (passive)
- ✅ "Update your address" (active)
- ❌ "Your address should be updated" (passive)

**When passive voice is OK**:
- When the actor is unknown or irrelevant: "Your card was charged $50"
- When you want to avoid blaming the user: "The password was entered incorrectly"

## Contractions
Use contractions to sound conversational and human.

- ✅ "We'll send you a notification"
- ✅ "You're all set"
- ✅ "It's on the way"
- ❌ "We will send you a notification" (too formal)

## Avoid Jargon
Skip technical jargon unless necessary for clarity.

- ✅ "Connect your bank" → ❌ "Link your financial institution"
- ✅ "Your money is protected" → ❌ "Your funds are secured via encryption protocols"

## Point of View

**Use "You/Your"** — address the user directly:
- ✅ "Your payment is complete"
- ✅ "Update your settings"
- ❌ "The user's payment is complete"

**Use "We"** — when the company/product is acting:
- ✅ "We sent you a code"
- ✅ "We couldn't process your payment"
- ❌ "Cash App sent you a code" (too third-person)

**Gender-neutral language** — use "they/them" for singular unknown persons:
- ✅ "When someone sends you money, they'll need your $cashtag"
- ❌ "When someone sends you money, he or she will need..."

## Reading Level and Sentence Length

**Target reading level**: 7th-8th grade for consumer products, 9th-10th for professional tools

**Sentence length**: 8-14 words for maximum comprehension
- Vary length for flow
- Break up long sentences
- One idea per sentence

- ✅ "We sent your payment. It'll arrive in 1-3 days."
- ❌ "We sent your payment and it should arrive in your bank account within 1-3 business days depending on your bank's processing speed."
