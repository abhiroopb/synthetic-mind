# Marketing Dates, Times, and Locations

AP Style baseline. Always follow localization guidance for non-US markets.

## Dates

**Specific dates** — abbreviate months (Jan., Feb., Aug., Sept., Oct., Nov., Dec. only); no ordinals
- ✅ "Jan. 20" ❌ "Jan. 20th"
- Day + date: "Sunday, February 23, at 4 PM" (comma after day, comma after date, space before time)
- Time: "4 PM" ✅ not "4 p.m."

**Vague dates** — spell out months
- ✅ "January 2018" ❌ "Jan. 2018"

## Locations

**Physical addresses** — abbreviate where possible
- ✅ "Sunset Blvd." ❌ "Sunset Boulevard"
- Exception: spell out for formal invitations (black-tie events)

**State regions** — lowercase compass point unless widely known section
- ✅ "central Louisiana", "Southern California", "East Coast"
- ❌ "East Michigan" (capitalize only widely known regions)

**Downtown** — capitalize for known neighborhood/district; lowercase for general region
- ✅ "Downtown Los Angeles" (known district) / "head downtown" (general direction)

**States** — spell out in paragraph form; abbreviations OK in bulleted city lists
- ✅ "Sacramento, California" in running text; "Sacramento, CA" in bullet list
- Use semicolons for lists of 3+ city/state pairs (not commas)
- ✅ "Dothan, Alabama; Sacramento, California; Destin, Florida"
- ❌ "Louisville, Kentucky, Honolulu, Hawaii, and Baton Rouge, Louisiana" (use semicolons)
