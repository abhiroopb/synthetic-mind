# Arcade: Cells, Modals, and Toasts

## Cell Components

**Labels**: Clear identifiers, brief noun/noun phrases — avoid articles unless they add clarity
**Body text**: Additional description or metadata; no punctuation unless complete sentences
**Icons**: Use for clarity and recognition — not decoration

**With selection controls**: Follow checkbox/radio/toggle guidelines; label serves as primary control text

**Product examples**:
- Brand B: "Payment methods", "Security settings", "Account preferences"
- Brand A: "Business settings", "Payment processing", "Employee management"
- Brand C: "Payment plans", "Purchase history", "Account settings"

---

## Modals

**Structure**: Title (25-40 chars) + Body (~100 chars, 2-3 lines) + Button(s) (~20 chars)

**Titles**:
- Brief statement of need or cause — no end punctuation unless a question
- Confirmation titles: "Delete account?" not "Are you sure?"
- No exclamation marks

**Body**:
- Complete sentences with end punctuation
- Be clear about consequences (upfront principle)
- Offer solutions when possible

**Buttons**:
- Specific actions, not "Yes/No"
- "Not now" for secondary dismissal
- Avoid "Cancel" in exit modals — use "Continue" or "Finish"

**Product examples**:
- Brand B → Title: "Delete payment method?" | Body: "You won't be able to use this card." | Buttons: "Delete" / "Keep"
- Brand A → Title: "Close register?" | Body: "Unsaved transactions will be lost." | Buttons: "Close" / "Continue"

---

## Toasts / Snackbars

**Structure**: Auto-dismiss confirmation messages

**Character limits**: Header 75 chars (50 chars if using body) | Body 75 chars

**Content rules**:
- Past tense verb phrases for confirmations
- Short phrases or fragments — not complete sentences
- No end punctuation anywhere

**Product examples**:
- Brand B: "Payment sent", "Money added", "Card activated"
- Brand A: "Sale processed", "Inventory updated", "Report generated"
- Brand C: "Payment scheduled", "Plan updated", "Purchase confirmed"

❌ Avoid: "Your payment has been sent successfully", "Success!"

---

## Lists

**Ordered**: Use for sequential instructions, steps, or priority order
**Unordered**: Use for related content where order doesn't matter — feature lists, benefits, related info
