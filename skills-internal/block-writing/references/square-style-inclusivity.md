# Square Style: Inclusivity, Technology, and Localization

## Inclusivity and Empathy

**Approach**: Sellers are from all walks of life—some thriving, some struggling. Don't overgeneralize.

Before publishing, ask:
- How would a seller doing well perceive this?
- How would a seller who's struggling perceive this?
- What groups are we excluding?

## Race and Ethnicity
- Always default to a person's preferred self-description—ask
- Prefer **BIPOC** over "minority" (avoid "minority"—it "others" people)
- **Asian vs. Asian-American**: distinguish; specify when possible (Indian-American, Taiwanese-American, etc.)
- ❌ "Oriental"
- Use **Black** (not "African-American") unless the context or the person's preference calls for it
- Avoid "black" and "white" as nouns; OK in statistical breakdowns; capitalize: Black
- **Latino**: Latin-American origin; **Hispanic**: descendants from Spain (use specific when possible—Puerto Rican, etc.); **Latinx**: gender-neutral alternative

## Pronouns, Sex, and Gender
- Use **LGBTQ+** for the whole community
- Always ask for pronouns: "What are your pronouns?" (not "preferred pronouns")
- **Transgender**: always an adjective, never a noun; use chosen name; avoid deadnaming
- ❌ cross-dressing, cross-dresser, she-male, he-she, trannie, tranny, gender bender, sex change operation, transvestite, pre-operative, post-operative
- Use "transition" instead of pre-operative/post-operative
- Refer to GLAAD's media reference guide for authoritative guidance

## Abilities
- Many Deaf community members prefer lowercase "d" for audiological status, uppercase "D" for culture/community
- Preferred terms: "Deaf" or "hard of hearing" (avoid "hearing impaired" unless self-identified)
- Follow NCDJ guidance

## Religion
Atheism is not capitalized; Buddhism, Christianity, Judaism are.
✅ "She is an atheist but her mom is a Buddhist."

## Technology Guidelines

**Website addresses**
- Use "at" with URLs, not "on": "Apply at square.com" ✅
- All lowercase, no www or http: "square.com" ✅
- Place hyperlinks on the action; don't include the period in the link

**Twitter / X** — "Twitter" capitalized; "tweet" lowercase; tweets are still tweets after rebrand

**Email** — All lowercase (meow@meowmeow.com); "email" not "e-mail"

**Android products** — Include ™ after first mention of "Android" or "Google Play"; attribution: "Android is a trademark of Google Inc."
- Apps available "on" the Google Play store (not "in")

**Apple products**
- Prefer "Download our app for iPhone" over "Download our iPhone app"
- Begin device names with lowercase 'i' even at sentence start (iPhone ✅)
- Apple preferred first: "iPhone or Android" ✅ not "Android and iPhone"
- Use "/" is fine for app stores: "on the App Store" ✅; not "Apple App Store" or "iTunes App Store"
- Apps are "on" or "from" the App Store (not "in")

## Legal and Localization

**Copyright** — Two versions: full (major/official assets) and condensed (lightweight assets); consult legal if unsure

**Localization considerations**
- Non-English text expands up to 30% in translation
- Partner with Localization early for context and intent
- Write clear, unambiguous, simple sentences; avoid long modifier strings
- Skip idioms, slang, culture-specific references ("home run", "9-to-5")
- Use consistent terminology throughout for easier translation

**Dynamic content and gender in translation**
- Spanish and French have grammatical gender—don't put dynamic variables inside sentences
- ❌ "Your business is growing in [Transportation / Professional Services]" (gender agreement breaks in Spanish)
- ✅ Separate dynamic content: "Your industry: [Transportation]" then a full sentence below
