# your company Voice Principles

Four foundational principles for all communication. Apply in order; **Upfront always wins** when they conflict.

## 1. Crisp — Say the most with the least
Lead with what matters most. Cut clutter, use tight language, front-load important information.

❌ "We wanted to let you know that your payment has been successfully processed and the funds should arrive in 1-3 business days, depending on your bank."
✅ "Payment sent. It'll arrive in 1-3 business days."

## 2. Self-assured — Say it like you mean it
Direct, declarative language. Skip hype and hedging. Let results speak.

Avoid: "might", "possibly", "try to", "we think you might enjoy"

❌ "If you're having issues, you can try contacting support and they might be able to help."
✅ "Contact support for help."

## 3. Fluent — Say it so it lands
Adapt tone to the moment. Sound like a real person, not a brand.

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| High-stakes (security, large transactions) | Serious, transparent, reassuring; more formal but still human |
| Routine tasks (balance check, small transactions) | Efficient, direct, minimal |
| First-time / onboarding | Patient, explanatory, slightly more detailed |
| Errors | Empathetic, solution-focused, never blame users |
| Success | Positive, specific, proportional — don't over-celebrate routine actions |

✅ "Your account has been temporarily locked to protect your money. Here's what to do next." (high-stakes)
✅ "Balance: $247.89" (routine)
✅ "Money sent to Alex" — not "Congratulations! You successfully completed your transaction!"

## 4. Upfront — Say the hard thing
Start with the truth. Be specific about outcomes and limitations. Own mistakes.

❌ "We're experiencing some technical difficulties at the moment."
✅ "Brand B isn't working right now. We're fixing it. Your money is safe."

❌ "We made a mistake and some payments were delayed."
✅ "We made a mistake and some payments were delayed. We've fixed it and your money is on the way. Sorry about that."

## Applying All Four Together

**Example: Account closure notification**
> "Your account will be closed on March 15. This is because we couldn't verify your identity. If you think this is a mistake, contact support by March 10."
- **Upfront**: leads with the important news
- **Crisp**: brief and clear
- **Self-assured**: no hedging
- **Fluent**: serious but respectful tone

## When Principles Conflict

1. **Upfront always wins**: never sacrifice transparency
2. **Crisp + Fluent**: be brief, but not so brief you sound robotic
3. **Self-assured + Fluent**: be confident, but adapt tone to the situation's seriousness

Good balance: "We couldn't send your payment. Your card was declined. Try another card or add money to your Brand B balance." — upfront, crisp, self-assured, and fluent.
