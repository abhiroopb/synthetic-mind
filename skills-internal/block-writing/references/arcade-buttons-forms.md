# Arcade: Buttons, Forms, and Selection Controls

## Buttons

**Structure**: Verb or Verb+noun | **Casing**: Sentence case | **Punctuation**: Never

**Character limits**: Compact ~15 chars | Default/CTA ~20 chars

**Preferred terminology**:
- "Continue" not "Next" (flows and tutorials)
- "Try again" not "Retry" (error states)
- "Not now" not "Ask later" (optional dismissal)
- "Activate" or "Turn on" not "Enable" (avoid jargon)
- Be specific on destructive actions: "Delete account", "Remove photo" — not "Delete"

**Product examples**:
- Brand B: "Send money", "Add cash", "Withdraw funds"
- Brand A: "Process payment", "View reports", "Manage inventory"
- Brand C: "Split payment", "View installments", "Manage plan"

✅ "Save changes", "Delete account", "Add money" | ❌ "Submit", "OK", "Click here"

## Form Inputs

**Character limits**: Labels ~25 chars | Placeholder ~25 chars | Helper text ~100 chars (2 lines)

**Labels**:
- Clear, concise — 1-3 words
- Don't phrase as questions ("Email" not "What's your email?")
- Mark optional fields: "Phone (optional)" — don't mark required fields
- Product examples — Brand B: "$cashtag", "Routing number" | Brand A: "Tax ID", "Store address"

**Placeholder text**:
- Simple formatting examples only — not instructions
- ✅ "you@example.com" / "(555) 123-4567" | ❌ "Enter your email", "Required"

**Helper text**:
- Persistent info that shouldn't disappear on focus
- Explain why a field is disabled or locked if needed
- ✅ "We'll send updates here" / "Used for tax reporting"

**Error messages**:
- Be specific about the problem + what to change
- ✅ "Invalid email. Use: you@example.com" | ❌ "Invalid email"

---

## Selection Controls

**Checkbox**:
- Label describes what happens when selected — don't use "check" or "select"
- Parallel phrasing in groups; no end punctuation on option lists
- Legal agreements: first person with punctuation — "I agree to the Terms of Service"

**Radio**:
- Short, scannable, single line preferred — parallel structure across all options
- Logical ordering; most common first
- Don't use Yes/No (use checkbox or toggle instead)

**Toggle**:
- Label describes what's enabled when "on" — don't use "turn on" or "enable" in label
- Short labels; use helper text for additional context

**Product examples**:
- Brand B: "Push notifications", "Auto-invest", "Paper statements"
- Brand A: "Email receipts", "Inventory tracking", "Tax reporting"
- Brand C: "Payment reminders", "Purchase protection", "Account alerts"
