# Accessibility: Inclusive Language

## Gender-Neutral Language
Use gender-neutral terms and singular "they."

**Pronouns**:
- ✅ "When someone sends you money, they'll need your $cashtag"
- ✅ "Contact support and they'll help"
- ❌ "When someone sends you money, he or she will need..."

**Job titles and roles**:
- ✅ Businessperson, business owner (not businessman/businesswoman)
- ✅ Server, waitstaff (not waiter/waitress)
- ✅ Sales representative (not salesman/saleswoman)
- ✅ Chair, chairperson (not chairman/chairwoman)

## Avoid Ableist Language
Don't use disability-related terms as metaphors.

- ✅ "Ignoring this issue" (not "turning a blind eye")
- ✅ "Unresponsive" (not "tone-deaf")
- ✅ "Unable to understand" (not "crippled by confusion")
- ✅ "Limiting belief" (not "crippling belief")

## Age-Neutral Language
Avoid assumptions about age or generational stereotypes.

- ✅ "People who prefer digital banking"
- ❌ "Millennials love mobile banking"
- ✅ "New to Brand B?" (not "Young and savvy?")

## Culturally Inclusive Language

**Avoid idioms and culture-specific references** that may not translate:
- ✅ "Very easy" (not "piece of cake")
- ✅ "Common" (not "run of the mill")
- ✅ "Get started" (not "hit the ground running")

**Be mindful of global audiences**:
- Use international date formats or spell out dates
- Consider currency symbols and formats
- Avoid US-centric assumptions (e.g., not everyone has a zip code)

## Socioeconomically Inclusive Language
Avoid assumptions about financial status or privilege.

- ✅ "Start saving" (not "grow your wealth")
- ✅ "Banking for everyone" (not "premium banking experience")
- ✅ "Access your money" (not "manage your fortune")

## Avoid Biased Language

**Criminal justice system**:
- ✅ "Person who was incarcerated" or "formerly incarcerated person"
- ❌ "Criminal," "convict," "felon" (stigmatizing)

**Housing status**:
- ✅ "Person experiencing homelessness"
- ❌ "Homeless person" (person-first language preferred)

**Substance use**:
- ✅ "Person with substance use disorder"
- ❌ "Addict," "junkie" (stigmatizing)
