# Square Style: Grammar and Mechanics

## Usage
- **Square** is a noun, not a verb. "Square's" may be used sparingly as a possessive.
- Baseline: AP Style unless specified here. Always follow localization guidance for non-US markets.

## Punctuation

**Oxford comma** — Use it: "planes, trains, and cars" ✅ (Most non-US markets do not use it)

**Em/en dashes**
- En-dash (-): ranges, compound adjectives; Em-dash (—): break up a phrase; include spaces around em-dash
- ✅ "pizza — but I chose salad" ❌ "pizza--but" or "pizza—but" (no spaces)

**Emojis** — Use in social/email marketing; avoid in product, support, and informational content
- End of complete sentence: punctuation precedes emoji. End of incomplete sentence: no punctuation.
- Accessibility: emojis are read by screen readers—don't overuse.

**Exclamation points** — Avoid; only when genuinely needed

**Asterisks** — Rare; place outside punctuation at end of sentence; use superscripts for legal disclaimers

**Ampersands** — Use sparingly to save space or for combined words (R&B, Q&A — no spaces)

**Footnotes/superscripts** — Numerical, beginning with 1; superscript after comma/full stop, before colon/semicolon
- Running text: `This is an example.¹ Next sentence.` ✅ — not `This is an example¹.`
- Footnote: `¹This is an example.` ✅ — not `¹ This is an example.` (no space)

**Acronyms** — Spell out on first use with parenthetical: "Work From Home (WFH)". Avoid periods (US not U.S.)

## Spacing
Never double space after a period or before a sentence.

## Capitalization
- Capitalize proper names of nationalities, peoples, races, tribes
- **Stat + descriptor pairs**: sentence case — "15M+ appointments booked monthly" ✅ not "15M+ Appointments Booked Monthly"

## Dates, Times, and Locations

**Specific dates** — Abbreviate months for specific dates (Jan., Feb., Aug., Sept., Oct., Nov., Dec.); no "th" or "st"
- ✅ "Jan. 20" ❌ "Jan. 20th"; ✅ "January 2018" (vague dates spell out)
- Day + date: "Sunday, February 23, at 4 PM" (commas); time: PM not p.m.

**Addresses** — Abbreviate where possible (Sunset Blvd. ✅); spell out for formal invitations

**States** — Spell out in paragraph form; use semicolons for lists of 3+ city/state pairs
- Abbreviations OK in bulleted city lists

**Compass points** — Lowercase ("central Louisiana") unless widely known section ("Southern California")

## Numbers

**In-product copy, headlines, subject lines, SMS** — Always use numerals.

**Numerals vs. spelled out**
- Inanimate objects, animals, specific people: always numerals (3-year-old house ✅)
- Standard AP: spell out zero through nine; 10+ use numerals
- Beginning a sentence: always spell out ("Eleven people watched")
- Emphasis numbers: numerals ("Get a loan in 2 hours" ✅)

**Percentages** — Always use % symbol; never spell out "percent"

**Fractions** — Spell out in running text (one-half); use numerals with whole numbers (1 1/2 cups)

**Measurements** — Numerals with units; space between numeral and unit (6 ft); don't pluralize abbreviations (10 lb not lbs)

**Years** — '80s, '90s (not 80's, 90's)

**Phone numbers** — (xxx) xxx-xxxx ✅ not 888-888-8888

**Multipliers** — Lowercase x: 2x, 3x ✅ not 2X, 3X

## Currency

**Dollars** — No commas for $1000–$9000; commas for $10,000+
- ✅ "up to $5000" ✅ "over $15,000" ❌ "$64000"

**Cents** — Use cent sign (¢) if space limited; spell out "cents" where space allows; use dollar sign for over $1
